United States (16 events)

🇺🇸 USA — FinCEN

What's happening: BSA/AML reform NPRM comment period closes June 9, 2026—three days from today—requiring financial institutions to fundamentally restructure AML/CFT programs under Bank Secrecy Act modernization and AML Act of 2020 implementation.

Deal play: Banks entering crypto custody and VASPs expanding banking partnerships need immediate compliance gap assessment—ask whether their current transaction monitoring infrastructure can satisfy reformed BSA standards or if they're building from scratch. Reactor investigations and KYT program architecture become baseline requirements.

🇺🇸 USA — FinCEN, FDIC, NCUA (GENIUS Act)

What's happening: Three agencies published parallel stablecoin AML/CFT NPRMs in May creating first federal payment stablecoin issuer supervision framework. FDIC comment closes July 17, NCUA by July 17—all targeting Q3 final rules for implementation before year-end.

Deal play: Payment stablecoin issuers and banking partners face 60-day compliance buildout window before final rules lock. Ask whether their reserve monitoring, transaction surveillance, and sanctions screening infrastructure scales to banking-grade standards—KYT stablecoin flows and Data Solutions reserve transparency become regulatory table stakes, not optional enhancements.

🇺🇸 USA — DOJ, SEC, Treasury

What's happening: DOJ National Cryptocurrency Enforcement Team expansion targets DeFi protocols, mixer services, and sanctions evasion with enhanced international coordination. SEC opens comment period on DeFi broker-dealer registration requirements while Treasury consults on state-level AML/CFT uniformity principles.

Deal play: DeFi protocols and exchanges integrating DeFi liquidity face dual enforcement/registration pressure—ask whether they can attribute flows through non-custodial infrastructure and demonstrate sanctions compliance without traditional KYC. Reactor attribution and OSINT entity identification separate compliant platforms from enforcement targets.

🇺🇸 USA — OCC, SEC, State Regulators

What's happening: OCC reviewing digital custody guidance for national banks in Q3, SEC replacing SAB 121 crypto custody accounting rules by Q4, and Delaware plus multiple states advancing stablecoin licensing bills under banking frameworks through H2.

Deal play: Banks launching custody services and multi-state stablecoin issuers hit three-layer compliance buildout: federal custody standards, accounting treatment clarity, and state licensing synchronization. Alterya custody security assessments and KYT multi-jurisdiction monitoring become competitive differentiators as custody market opens.

Canada (3 events)

🇨🇦 Canada — CSA, FINTRAC, OSFI

What's happening: CSA implementing enhanced registration and custody requirements for crypto trading platforms in Q3. FINTRAC phasing Travel Rule compliance deadlines through Q4 beyond tax reporting. OSFI consulting on Group 2a tokenized asset risk weightings for cross-market hedge collateral recognition.

Deal play: Canadian platforms face triple compliance sprint: custody security uplift, Travel Rule counterparty screening, and capital treatment clarity for tokenized traditional assets. Ask whether their surveillance infrastructure handles real-time Travel Rule data exchange while maintaining audit trails for CSA inspections—KYT Travel Rule modules and Alterya custody frameworks compress Q3 buying decisions.

KYT licensing preparation and OSINT platform screening create competitive advantage for early movers.

Colombia — SFC

Deal play: Colombian VASPs transitioning from unregulated to supervised status need immediate compliance baseline establishment—Reactor entity verification and KYT AML program architecture become licensing prerequisites as SFC regime operationalizes.

Cayman Islands — CIMA

Deal play: Offshore funds and exchanges domiciled in Cayman face VASP registration requirements for first time—ask whether their compliance infrastructure meets CIMA standards or if they're building from zero. KYT fund monitoring and Reactor entity screening become mandatory for Cayman-registered platforms.
