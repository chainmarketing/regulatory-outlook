Executive Summary

A summary of the key regulatory developments across the region this quarter

Twenty-four Americas markets are operationalizing AML/CFT frameworks as U.S. federal agencies finalize GENIUS Act stablecoin supervision and fundamental BSA reform while states race to establish competing crypto licensing regimes. United States (16 events)

🇺🇸  — FinCEN

What's happening: BSA/AML reform NPRM fundamentally restructures financial institution compliance programs—public comment closes June 9 after April 10 Federal Register publication. Parallel GENIUS Act NPRM establishes stablecoin issuer AML/CFT supervision.

Deal play: Banks, stablecoin issuers, and MSBs need KYT program redesign and Reactor risk assessment infrastructure before comment window closes—ask whether their current BSA/AML architecture survives FinCEN's effectiveness-based framework shift or requires complete rebuild. We've already submitted positions on GENIUS Act implementation.

🇺🇸  — FDIC / NCUA

What's happening: Two GENIUS Act NPRMs establish AML/CFT standards for FDIC-supervised stablecoin issuers and credit union subsidiaries—FDIC comment period closes July 17, NCUA follows.

Deal play: Regional banks and credit unions eyeing stablecoin issuance need KYT transaction monitoring and OSINT reserve verification before Q3 comment deadlines—ask whether their current AML infrastructure scales to payment stablecoin velocity or requires dedicated monitoring layer.

🇺🇸  — SEC

What's happening: Three consultations moving: consolidated audit trail digital asset reporting requirements, DeFi broker-dealer registration guidance, and SAB 121 replacement on crypto custody accounting for banks.

Deal play: Exchanges and DeFi protocols need Data Solutions market surveillance and KYT protocol flow monitoring—ask whether their compliance team has capacity to respond to three simultaneous SEC consultations while maintaining existing surveillance obligations.

🇺🇸  — Treasury / State Regulators

What's happening: Treasury consultation on state-level AML/CFT uniformity principles addresses fragmented licensing landscape while Delaware, Wyoming, and multiple states advance competing stablecoin and money transmitter bills through H2.

Deal play: Multi-state crypto businesses need KYT multi-jurisdiction compliance infrastructure and Reactor state-by-state baseline monitoring—ask whether their current compliance architecture handles 50-state licensing fragmentation or collapses under conflicting reporting requirements.

🇺🇸  — Department of Justice

What's happening: National Cryptocurrency Enforcement Team expansion targets DeFi protocols, mixer services, and sanctions evasion with enhanced international coordination.

Deal play: DeFi platforms and privacy-focused protocols need Reactor sanctions screening and KYT mixer detection before DOJ enforcement wave hits—ask whether their current compliance posture withstands enhanced scrutiny or exposes them to enforcement risk.

🇺🇸  — OCC / Federal Reserve

What's happening: OCC reviewing digital asset custody guidance for national banks in Q3 while Federal Reserve explores FedNow crypto integration implications for stablecoin settlement infrastructure.

Deal play: National banks planning custody services need Alterya custody security assessment and KYT institutional flow monitoring—ask whether their custody infrastructure meets updated OCC standards before Q4 guidance release.
