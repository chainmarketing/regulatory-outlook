United States (16 events)

🇺🇸  — FinCEN

What's happening: Three parallel rulemakings converge: BSA/AML program reform (comment deadline June 9), GENIUS Act stablecoin issuer AML/CFT requirements (comment deadline July 17), and Treasury consultation on state-level crypto regime uniformity principles closing through June. Final BSA rule expected Q3 with 12-18 month implementation windows triggering immediate compliance infrastructure procurement.

Deal play: Financial institutions need KYT transaction monitoring overhauls to meet risk-based program standards while stablecoin issuers (Circle, Paxos, PayPal) require sanctions screening infrastructure before FinCEN supervision activates—ask whether their current vendor stack scales when beneficial ownership reporting hits Corporate Transparency Act registries and cross-state uniformity requirements force data standardization. Reactor case management becomes critical when DeFi attribution requirements expand.

🇺🇸  — DOJ / SEC

What's happening: DOJ Cryptocurrency Enforcement Team expansion targeting DeFi protocols and mixer services with international coordination, while SEC opens comment period on DeFi broker-dealer registration thresholds and consolidated audit trail requirements for digital asset transactions. Enforcement velocity accelerating following Delaware stablecoin licensing bill (September introduction) and OCC digital asset custody guidance update (Q3).

Deal play: Exchanges and DeFi front-ends face broker-dealer classification risk—Data Solutions protocol surveillance and KYT trading flow monitoring become insurance against enforcement. Custody banks awaiting OCC guidance need Alterya security assessments before SAB 121 replacement drops Q4.

🇺🇸  — FDIC / NCUA

What's happening: FDIC and NCUA parallel NPRMs implementing GENIUS Act AML/CFT standards for permitted payment stablecoin issuers supervised by banking regulators—NCUA comment period closes July 17 with credit union subsidiaries facing compliance framework identical to bank-issued stablecoins.

Deal play: Regional banks and credit unions entering stablecoin issuance need KYT reserve monitoring and OSINT issuer due diligence before supervision activates—smaller institutions lack enterprise compliance infrastructure, creating wedge opportunity against incumbent banking vendors.

Canada (3 events)

Deal play: Banks holding tokenized securities need Data Solutions classification infrastructure to demonstrate regulatory-compliant asset categorization—OSFI consultation response deadline unclear but institutions should prepare position papers now using Chainalysis attribution data.

Latin America & Caribbean (5 events)

Chile — CMF

What's happening: CMF developing implementing regulations for crypto service providers under 2023 Fintech Law (Law 21.521)—licensing and operational framework for virtual asset platforms finalizing through December with AML/CFT requirements mirroring FATF standards.

Deal play: Chilean exchanges and fintech platforms need KYT transaction monitoring and Reactor entity verification before licensing applications open Q4—ask whether they've mapped Fintech Law AML requirements to their current compliance stack or waiting until CMF publishes final rules (too late for procurement cycles).

Colombia — SFC

What's happening: SFC finalizing comprehensive regulatory framework for crypto asset service providers—central bank draft law moving from legal gray area to defined licensing regime with AML/CFT requirements aligning to regional enforcement standards through Q2-Q3.

Deal play: Colombian VASPs operating in regulatory ambiguity face licensing sprint once SFC publishes framework—KYT platform monitoring and OSINT entity screening become table stakes for registration, creating urgency window before enforcement separates compliant platforms from exits.

Argentina — CNV

What's happening: CNV crypto asset service provider registration regime implementing phased compliance deadlines through Q4 with AML/CFT requirements establishing transaction monitoring obligations for licensed platforms.

Deal play: Argentine exchanges need KYT compliance infrastructure and Reactor AML program documentation before CNV registration windows close—ask whether they've budgeted for ongoing monitoring costs or assuming one-time licensing spend suffices (it doesn't).

Cayman Islands — CIMA

What's happening: CIMA Virtual Asset Service Provider framework implementing through Q4 with registration and AML compliance requirements establishing Cayman as Caribbean crypto hub—offshore funds and service providers face licensing deadlines.

Deal play: Cayman-domiciled crypto funds and custodians need KYT counterparty screening and OSINT beneficial ownership verification for CIMA registration—institutional clients demand Cayman licensing, making compliance infrastructure non-negotiable for fund administrators and prime brokers.
