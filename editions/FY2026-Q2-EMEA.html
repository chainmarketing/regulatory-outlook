Executive Summary

The big picture — where things stand right now

EMEA is executing the world's largest synchronized crypto regulatory activation through September 2026. The EU's MiCA framework transitions from law to operational enforcement as AMLA begins direct supervision of high-risk CASPs across 27 member states, while 211 firms have already secured authorization and 149 remain non-compliant. Parallel momentum in UK stablecoin frameworks, Middle East VASP licensing regimes, and emerging African regulatory structures creates immediate compliance infrastructure demand worth hundreds of millions across exchanges, banks, and institutional service providers.

European Union (21 events)

🇪🇺 EU — ESMA / AMLA

What's happening: AMLA launches Q3 2026 with direct supervision powers over highest-risk CASPs across EU27; MiCA Level 2 RTS packages on custody, operational resilience, and stablecoin reserves publish Q2-Q4; MiCA review consultation (Aug 31 deadline) assesses DeFi, NFTs, and CASP requirements; privacy coin ban and anonymous transaction prohibition under AMLR takes effect July 2027.

Deal play: CASPs need KYT for AMLA-grade transaction monitoring and Reactor for cross-border flow attribution—ask: "How will you demonstrate Level 2 custody and AML compliance when AMLA begins direct examinations in Q3?" We've submitted positions on AMLA group-wide standards and are monitoring MiCA review closely.

🇫🇷 France — AMF

What's happening: PSAN firms must transition to full MiCA authorization by July 1, 2026—legacy registrations expire and firms without submitted applications must cease operations.

Deal play: PSAN providers need KYT baseline compliance and Reactor for authorization documentation—ask: "What transaction monitoring architecture are you presenting to AMF for MiCA authorization before July 1?"

🇩🇪 Germany — BaFin

What's happening: BaFin enforcing ongoing crypto custody license requirements under Banking Act with active supervision of MiCA-authorized firms through 2026.

Deal play: Custody providers need Alterya for vault security and KYT for custodial flow monitoring—ask: "How are you satisfying BaFin's custody standards under both national Banking Act and MiCA simultaneously?"

🇳🇱 Netherlands — DNB

Deal play: Registered VASPs need KYT for Travel Rule counterparty verification and Reactor for DNB reporting—ask: "Can you demonstrate Travel Rule compliance and originator tracing to DNB auditors today?"
