Executive Summary

The big picture — where things stand right now

EMEA regulators are executing the world's most coordinated crypto compliance infrastructure buildout—211 authorized CASPs across 23 EU countries face MiCA Level 2 technical standards through September while AMLA becomes operational with direct supervision powers in Q3. The UK's FCA enforces September authorization deadlines, France's July 1 PSAN cutoff creates urgent compliance demand, and Middle Eastern regulators finalize comprehensive licensing frameworks. This synchronized regulatory maturation across 35+ jurisdictions creates immediate compliance infrastructure demand for KYT, Reactor, Alterya, and Hexagate deployments.

European Union (21 events)

🇪🇺 EU — AMLA / ESMA / EC

What's happening: AMLA becomes operational Q3 with direct supervision over highest-risk CASPs; ESMA publishing MiCA Level 2 RTS packages Q2-Q4 on custody, stablecoins, operational resilience, and market abuse; MiCA review consultation opens August examining DeFi, NFTs, and CASP requirements; DORA operationalization begins July; privacy coin ban and anonymous transaction prohibition effective July 2027.

Deal play: Highest-risk CASPs need KYT and Reactor to demonstrate continuous compliance monitoring before AMLA direct supervision begins. The MiCA review consultation (Aug 31 deadline) creates demand for Data Solutions to help CASPs position themselves for DeFi and NFT regulatory clarity. DORA's July operationalization requires Alterya for cybersecurity incident reporting and operational resilience. We've already submitted consultation responses on AMLA group-wide AML standards and ESMA/EBA suitability guidelines.

🇫🇷 France — AMF

What's happening: French crypto firms operating under PSAN registration must transition to full MiCA authorization by July 1, 2026, or cease operations. AMF is issuing public warnings to non-compliant firms.

Deal play: Every PSAN-registered firm needs KYT and Reactor to demonstrate continuous transaction monitoring and entity verification for MiCA authorization applications. This is a hard cutoff creating immediate Q3 compliance spend.

🇩🇪 Germany — BaFin

What's happening: BaFin actively supervising and enforcing crypto custody license requirements under German Banking Act with ongoing compliance expectations throughout 2026.

Deal play: German custody providers (55 authorized CASPs, the most in the EU) need Alterya for custody security and KYT for institutional surveillance to maintain BaFin licensing.

🇳🇱 Netherlands — DNB

What's happening: Dutch central bank enhancing supervision of registered crypto firms with focus on AML compliance and Travel Rule implementation throughout Q2-Q3.

Deal play: Dutch exchanges and VASPs (25 authorized CASPs) need KYT for Travel Rule screening and Reactor for counterparty verification as DNB intensifies supervision.
