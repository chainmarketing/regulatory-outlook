Executive Summary

A summary of the key regulatory developments across the region this quarter

The European Union is executing the largest coordinated crypto compliance buildout in history—211 authorized CASPs across 23 countries face MiCA Level 2 technical standards while AMLA becomes operational with direct supervision powers in Q3. Simultaneously, the UK's FCA is closing its authorization gateway with firm Q3 deadlines, France enforces its July 1 PSAN transition cutoff, and Middle Eastern regulators in UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Israel finalize comprehensive licensing frameworks. This represents a synchronized regulatory maturation across 35+ EMEA jurisdictions, creating immediate compliance infrastructure demand.

European Union (21 events)

🇪🇺 EU — AMLA / ESMA / EC

What's happening: AMLA becomes operational Q3 with direct supervision over highest-risk CASPs while ESMA publishes MiCA Level 2 RTS packages through Q4 on custody standards, stablecoin reserves, operational resilience (DORA), and market abuse. Three consultations opened: MiCA review (Aug 31), AMLA group-wide AML/CFT standards (May 26), and anti-corruption strategy (July).

Deal play: The 211 authorized CASPs need KYT transaction monitoring that scales to AMLA's direct supervision scrutiny—ask whether their current stack can handle supervisor-grade reporting requirements. Reactor investigations become critical for demonstrating robust source-of-funds verification. The MiCA review consultation explicitly covers DeFi, NFTs, and stablecoins—firms submitting responses need Data Solutions intelligence on market structure trends and Hexagate security assessments for DeFi protocol compliance.

🇫🇷 France — AMF

What's happening: July 1 deadline for PSAN-to-MiCA transitions now active enforcement priority—firms operating under pre-MiCA registration must complete full authorization or cease operations.

Deal play: French exchanges and custodians rushing to meet July 1 need immediate KYT baseline compliance and Alterya custody security assessments to pass AMF's enhanced authorization requirements.

🇩🇪 Germany — BaFin

What's happening: BaFin actively supervising and enforcing crypto custody license requirements under German Banking Act with ongoing compliance expectations throughout 2026.

Deal play: German custodians need Alterya to demonstrate secure key management and operational controls that satisfy BaFin's banking-grade custody standards—ask whether their current custody setup would pass a BaFin audit.

🇳🇱 Netherlands — DNB

What's happening: Dutch central bank enhancing supervision of registered crypto firms with focus on AML compliance and Travel Rule implementation in Q2.

Deal play: Dutch VASPs need KYT with comprehensive Travel Rule compliance to meet DNB's enhanced supervision standards—ask how they're currently handling cross-border Travel Rule messages for CASP-to-CASP transfers.

🇳🇴 Norway — Finanstilsynet

What's happening: Money Laundering Act amendments for crypto extend AML requirements to broader range of crypto activities with enhanced VASP supervision in Q3.

Deal play: Norwegian firms expanding into new crypto services need KYT that covers the extended AML scope—ask whether their transaction monitoring captures all activities now falling under the amended Money Laundering Act.

🇪🇸 Spain — Bank of Spain

What's happening: Bank of Spain enforcing strict advertising rules for crypto products requiring authorization and risk warnings for marketing campaigns throughout 2026.

Deal play: Spanish exchanges need OSINT monitoring to ensure marketing campaigns comply with Bank of Spain advertising authorization requirements—ask how they're currently tracking and verifying crypto product marketing compliance.

🇸🇪 Sweden — FI

What's happening: Finansinspektionen implementing MiCA with enhanced national AML measures for crypto firms beyond EU minimums in H2.

Deal play: Swedish CASPs need KYT that exceeds baseline MiCA requirements to satisfy FI's enhanced national AML standards—ask whether their current transaction monitoring meets Sweden's stricter thresholds.

United Kingdom (6 events)

🇬🇧 UK — FCA / BoE / HMT

What's happening: FCA consultation on final crypto authorization rules closes June with firm Q3 deadlines for existing businesses to submit applications under the financial services gateway. Law Commission bill recognizing crypto as property under English law advances H2, enabling improved legal remedies for theft and fraud. Bank of England developing separate Q4 framework for fiat-backed stablecoins as payment instruments. FCA and BoE joint call for input on tokenisation in wholesale markets opens July.

Deal play: UK crypto firms facing Q3 authorization deadlines need KYT transaction monitoring and Reactor investigations ready for FCA gateway submissions—ask whether their compliance stack demonstrates the "conduct standards, systems and controls" the FCA consultation specifies. The Law Commission property recognition creates immediate demand for Reactor asset tracing in theft/fraud cases. Stablecoin issuers preparing for Q4 payment framework need KYT payment flow monitoring. The tokenisation call for input creates opportunity for Data Solutions intelligence on DLT infrastructure and post-trade processes.
