European Union (39 events)

🇪🇺 EU — EC/ESMA/AMLA

What's happening: Three massive deadlines converge: MiCA implementation goes live July 1 requiring all CASPs to comply with operational resilience, market abuse, custody, and stablecoin reserve standards; AMLA becomes operational Q3 2026 with direct supervision authority over highest-risk crypto providers across EU27; EU AI Act full application August 2026 covering high-risk financial sector systems (AML, fraud detection, credit scoring) with conformity assessments and EU database registration required. DORA operationalisation by firms continues through July. Sequential publication of 30+ regulatory technical standards through December 2027 covering CASP compliance, data formats, and market surveillance requirements. European Commission consultation on MiCA review closes August 2026, assessing DeFi, NFTs, stablecoins, and environmental impact — may trigger legislative amendments. AMLA consultations on group-wide AML/CFT requirements and criteria for enhanced due diligence closed May 2026. Privacy coin ban under Anti-Money Laundering Regulation takes effect July 2027 — all transactions require full identity verification and traceability.

Deal play: Every EU CASP needs three things immediately: KYT for real-time transaction monitoring against MiCA market abuse standards, Reactor for custody audit trails proving asset segregation, and Data Solutions for RTS reporting obligations. Ask: "Have you mapped which of the 30+ technical standards apply to your license category, and do you have surveillance infrastructure ready for AMLA direct supervision?" We've already submitted positions on AMLA RTS criteria and are engaging on MiCA review DeFi provisions.

🇫🇷 France — AMF

What's happening: French PSAN providers must transition to MiCA authorisation by July 1, 2026 — AMF urging firms to begin applications immediately as deadline approaches. PSAN registration (operational since 2020) becomes insufficient; full MiCA licensing with enhanced operational resilience, systems/controls, and custody requirements now mandatory for all crypto-asset services.

Deal play: Every PSAN-registered firm needs compliance infrastructure upgrade → KYT for MiCA-grade transaction monitoring, Alterya for custody segregation requirements. Ask: "Have you started your MiCA authorisation application, and can your current systems meet the enhanced operational resilience standards?"

🇩🇪 Germany — BaFin

What's happening: BaFin actively supervising and enforcing crypto custody license requirements under German Banking Act (Kreditwesengesetz) with ongoing compliance expectations — Germany maintains parallel national custody regime alongside MiCA implementation, creating dual compliance burden for custodians.

Deal play: German custodians need Alterya for both BaFin custody licensing and MiCA compliance, plus Reactor for audit trail documentation. Ask: "Are you meeting both BaFin's KWG custody requirements and preparing for MiCA's additional operational standards?"

🇳🇱 Netherlands — DNB

What's happening: Dutch central bank enhancing supervision of registered crypto firms with focus on AML compliance and Travel Rule implementation — active enforcement phase beyond initial registration, targeting transaction monitoring effectiveness and cross-border information sharing.

Deal play: Dutch VASPs upgrading from registration to full supervision need KYT for Travel Rule compliance (originator/beneficiary information) and Reactor for counterparty verification. Ask: "Can your current transaction monitoring system handle DNB's enhanced AML supervision expectations?"

🇳🇴 Norway — Finanstilsynet

What's happening: Money Laundering Act amendments for crypto expected Q3 2026 — extending AML requirements to broader range of crypto activities and establishing enhanced VASP supervision framework beyond current registration.

Deal play: Norwegian crypto firms preparing for expanded AML scope need KYT for broader transaction coverage and OSINT for enhanced customer due diligence. Ask: "When the Money Laundering Act amendments take effect, will your compliance infrastructure cover the expanded activity definitions?"

🇪🇸 Spain — Bank of Spain

What's happening: Bank of Spain enforcing strict advertising rules for crypto products requiring authorisation and risk warnings for marketing campaigns — active enforcement of consumer protection standards with penalties for non-compliant promotional materials.

Deal play: Spanish CASPs need compliance documentation proving transaction legitimacy supporting marketing claims → Data Solutions for defensible advertising substantiation. Ask: "Can you demonstrate to Bank of Spain that your marketing claims are backed by verifiable transaction data?"

🇸🇪 Sweden — Finansinspektionen

What's happening: FI implementing MiCA with enhanced national AML measures beyond EU minimums — Sweden layering additional crypto-specific AML requirements on top of MiCA baseline, creating stricter compliance environment than other member states.

Deal play: Swedish VASPs face dual compliance burden → KYT for both MiCA and enhanced national AML standards. Ask: "Are you prepared for FI's enhanced AML requirements that go beyond the MiCA minimums other EU countries are implementing?"

United Kingdom (16 events)

🇬🇧 UK — FCA/BoE/HMT

What's happening: Four critical milestones: FCA crypto authorisation gateway opens September 2026 with firm application deadlines for existing businesses — cryptoasset activities become regulated financial services requiring full authorisation. FCA consultation CP26/13 on perimeter guidance closed June 2026, defining which activities require authorisation (stablecoin issuance, custody, trading platforms, dealing, arranging, staking). HM Treasury draft SI amending FSMA 2000 carved UK-issued qualifying stablecoins out of dealing/arranging perimeter ahead of payments reforms. Systemic stablecoins must comply with Bank of England prudential regime from January 2027 including prudential requirements, operational resilience standards, and resolution planning. FCA/BoE joint call for input on tokenisation seeks views on securities, bonds, equities, fund units covering DLT infrastructure and regulatory clarity. Basel standards implementation H2 2026. AI Bill anticipated H2 2026 following Data Act 2025. CSAM Online Safety Act first statutory report October 2026. Law Commission bill recognising crypto as property enables improved legal remedies for theft/fraud. Enhanced ECCTA verification requirements affect crypto company registrations.

Deal play: Every UK crypto firm needs September gateway readiness → Reactor for custody authorisation evidence, KYT for transaction monitoring systems/controls, Alterya for operational resilience standards. Stablecoin issuers preparing for January 2027 prudential regime need Data Solutions for resolution planning and stress testing. Ask: "Have you submitted your FCA authorisation application, and can you demonstrate the systems and controls the gateway requires?" FCA consultation responses available.
