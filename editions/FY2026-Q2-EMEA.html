European Union (21 events)

🇪🇺 EU — AMLA / ESMA / EC

What's happening: AMLA becomes operational Q3 with direct supervision authority over highest-risk CASPs; ESMA publishing MiCA Level 2 RTS packages Q2-Q4 on custody, stablecoins, operational resilience (DORA), and market abuse; four consultations opened—MiCA review (Aug 31), AMLA group-wide AML/CFT standards, ESMA/EBA suitability guidelines for CASP management, and EC anti-corruption strategy (July). Privacy coin ban and anonymous crypto transaction prohibition takes effect July 2027 under new AMLR. NFT classification guidance expected December determining which activities require MiCA compliance.

Deal play: Every CASP under AMLA supervision needs KYT for continuous transaction monitoring and Reactor for entity attribution—direct supervision creates non-negotiable compliance baseline. MiCA review consultation closing Aug 31 creates Data Solutions opportunity: regulators need market structure intelligence on DeFi, NFTs, and stablecoin evolution. Privacy coin ban implementation requires OSINT for exchange delisting verification and KYT for protocol blocking.

🇫🇷 France — AMF

What's happening: July 1, 2026 deadline for PSAN-to-MiCA transition is now active enforcement priority—AMF issuing public warnings to firms still operating under pre-MiCA registration requiring full authorization or operations cessation.

Deal play: Every French PSAN provider (19 authorized CASPs in ESMA register) needs KYT upgrade for MiCA-compliant transaction monitoring and Alterya for enhanced custody standards—deadline creates 8-week compliance sprint. Ask: "Have you submitted your MiCA authorization application? What's your July 1 contingency if approval delays?"

🇩🇪 Germany — BaFin

What's happening: BaFin actively supervising and enforcing crypto custody license requirements under German Banking Act with ongoing compliance expectations through 2026.

Deal play: Germany leads EU with 55 authorized CASPs—largest addressable custody compliance market. Alterya positions against custody operational failures and KYT monitors institutional-grade transaction surveillance. Ask: "How are you demonstrating custody security to BaFin during their supervision cycles?"

🇳🇱 Netherlands — DNB

Deal play: KYT for Travel Rule compliance across counterparty VASPs and Reactor for Dutch entity verification—DNB supervision intensity creates compliance urgency. Ask: "What's your Travel Rule coverage percentage across counterparties?"

🇧🇪 Belgium — RUSI Brussels

Deal play: KYT for expanded AML scope and Reactor for Norwegian VASP entity attribution—legislative expansion creates new regulated population needing baseline compliance. Ask: "Which crypto activities fall under your new AML obligations?"

United Kingdom (6 events)

🇬🇧 UK — FCA / BoE / HMT

What's happening: FCA consultation on final crypto authorization rules closing June with firm Q3 deadlines for existing businesses to submit applications under financial services registration regime. Law Commission bill advancing H2 recognizing crypto as property under English law, enabling improved legal remedies for theft/fraud. Joint FCA/BoE call for input on tokenisation in wholesale markets opening July covering DLT infrastructure, securities tokenisation, and regulatory clarity. Separate Q4 stablecoin payment framework distinct from broader crypto authorization gateway. Enhanced Corporate Criminal Offences and Transparency Act (ECCTA) verification requirements enforced throughout 2026.

Deal play: Q3 authorization deadline creates urgent KYT deployment window for existing UK crypto businesses—FCA won't extend grace periods. Reactor positions for crypto-as-property legal remedies (theft/fraud recovery) and OSINT monitors ECCTA corporate transparency compliance. Tokenisation call for input creates Data Solutions opportunity: regulators need market structure intelligence on wholesale DLT adoption. Ask: "Have you submitted your FCA authorization application? What's your Q3 backup plan if you're not approved?"
