Executive Summary

The big picture — where things stand right now

Asia-Pacific enters the most synchronized compliance infrastructure activation in the region's history. Fourteen jurisdictions face simultaneous VASP licensing deadlines, institutional custody launches, and AML enforcement escalations through September 2026. Japan's reclassification of crypto as investment products and Vietnam's 0.1% transaction tax create immediate compliance infrastructure demand across exchanges, banks, and traditional financial institutions entering crypto markets.

What Changed Since Last Quarter

Looking back — what moved, resolved, or escalated last quarter

  • Japan 🏛️ JFSA reclassifying crypto from Payment Services Act to Financial Instruments and Exchange Act (H2 2026)—crypto becomes investment products with securities-grade regulation, triggering compliance infrastructure overhaul for all FSA-registered exchanges.
  • Vietnam ⚡ 0.1% transaction tax on all digital asset transactions live July 1, 2026; Law on Digital Technology Industry recognizes crypto as property; 5-year pilot trading market via licensed VASPs with VND 10T minimum capital—full market structure operationalization after years of prohibition.
  • Australia 📋 Treasury advancing Tranche 1 stablecoin payment service provider licensing consultation—crypto payment gateways regulated alongside traditional PSPs under reformed payments framework; AUSTRAC escalating Travel Rule enforcement from implementation to active compliance verification.
  • South Korea & Thailand 🆕 FSC finalizing Q4 institutional custody framework for banks serving crypto clients; Thai SEC expanding Q3 licensing categories for brokers/dealers with mandatory AML requirements and September travel rule activation—both creating urgent institutional compliance infrastructure gaps.
  • Singapore & Hong Kong 🔍 MAS launching July enhanced enforcement phase on FATF travel rule compliance with mandatory originator/beneficiary information above SGD 1,500; SFC preparing November tokenized securities platform authorization requirements with market surveillance standards—secondary trading infrastructure now requires exchange-grade oversight.
