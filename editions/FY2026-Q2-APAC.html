Executive Summary

The big picture — where things stand right now

Nine Asia-Pacific jurisdictions are simultaneously executing VASP licensing, institutional custody standards, and AML enforcement through September 2026. Australia's AUSTRAC switches from implementation to active Travel Rule enforcement while its Treasury consults on stablecoin payment licensing—the first major market to regulate crypto payment gateways under reformed PSP frameworks. Southeast Asian regulators face Q3 custody and registration deadlines creating immediate compliance infrastructure demand across exchanges, banks, and institutional crypto service providers worth hundreds of millions in aggregate spend.

What Changed Since Last Quarter

  • Australia ⚡ AUSTRAC moved from Travel Rule implementation phase to active enforcement Q2 2026; Treasury opened Tranche 1 consultation on stablecoin and crypto-payment gateway licensing under reformed payments framework—first major market to license crypto as PSPs.
  • South Korea & Thailand 🆕 FSC developing H2 2026 institutional custody framework allowing traditional banks to provide crypto custody to institutional clients; Thai SEC expanding Q3 licensing for brokers/dealers with enhanced AML requirements.
