Executive Summary

The big picture — where things stand right now

Asia-Pacific is executing the world's most diverse regulatory buildout—9 jurisdictions across four sub-regions are simultaneously finalizing VASP licensing frameworks, institutional custody standards, and AML enforcement regimes through Q3 2026. Australia's AUSTRAC shifts from implementation to active Travel Rule enforcement while Hong Kong, South Korea, and Thailand expand institutional access. Southeast Asian regulators are racing to implement custody standards and VASP registration before Q3 deadlines, creating urgent compliance infrastructure demand worth hundreds of millions across exchanges, banks, and emerging institutional crypto service providers.

What Changed Since Last Quarter

Looking back — what moved, resolved, or escalated last quarter

  • Australia ⚡ AUSTRAC transitioned from Travel Rule implementation to active enforcement phase Q2; Treasury consultation on stablecoin payment licensing opened H1, positioning Australia as first major market to regulate crypto payment gateways under reformed PSP framework.
  • Hong Kong 🔄 SFC continuing crypto ETF approvals with retail access expanding beyond initial April 2024 launches—institutional product diversification creating new market surveillance and custody compliance demand.
  • South Korea & Thailand 🆕 FSC developing institutional custody framework for traditional FIs (Q4 launch); Thai SEC expanding licensing categories for brokers and dealers with enhanced AML requirements (Q3 implementation).
  • Philippines, Indonesia ⚡ BSP enforcing ongoing VASP registration deadlines under Circular 1108; OJK implementing custody standards post-regulatory transfer from Bappebti—both creating immediate exchange compliance pressure.
  • India 🏛️ SEBI clarifying crypto securities jurisdiction vs. RBI in H2—regulatory boundary definition will determine which platforms require securities licensing vs. payment/commodity oversight.

Deal play: Brokers and dealers entering licensing need KYT for customer due diligence and travel rule compliance—ask: "Are you applying for expanded licensing categories and how are you implementing Thai SEC's enhanced AML requirements including travel rule verification?"
