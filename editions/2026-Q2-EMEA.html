European Union (21 events)

🇪🇺 EU — EC / ESMA / AMLA

What's happening: AMLA becomes operational Q3 2026 with direct supervision authority over the highest-risk crypto asset service providers across all 27 EU member states, marking the shift from national registration regimes to centralized EU-level enforcement. MiCA Level 2 Regulatory Technical Standards covering operational resilience, market abuse, custody, and stablecoin reserves are being adopted throughout Q2-Q4, creating detailed compliance obligations beyond the Level 1 framework.

So what for deals: Every CASP operating cross-border in the EU needs to demonstrate group-wide AML/CFT compliance, continuous transaction monitoring, and travel rule infrastructure before AMLA's risk-based supervision begins—ask prospects "How will you prove compliance to AMLA supervisors when they request transaction flow documentation?" The European Commission's MiCA Review consultation (closing August 31) seeks input on DeFi, NFTs, and stablecoin framework adjustments—responding positions us as regulatory thought leaders with EC policymakers.

Pipeline trigger: Banks, payment processors, and crypto exchanges with multi-country EU operations face AMLA's group-wide supervision—Reactor for entity resolution across subsidiaries, KYT for unified transaction monitoring, Data Solutions for market abuse surveillance across trading venues.

🇫🇷 France — AMF

What's happening: French crypto firms operating under PSAN registration must transition to full MiCA authorization by July 1, 2026, or cease operations. The AMF is urgently pushing firms to submit applications as the deadline approaches, with no grace period confirmed for firms in-process.

So what for deals: Every PSAN-registered firm (150+ entities) is in active procurement right now for transaction monitoring, wallet screening, and compliance infrastructure—ask "Have you submitted your MiCA authorization application yet, and what transaction monitoring system did you include in your application?" This is a closed-window opportunity: firms without systems operational by July 1 face operational shutdown.

🇩🇪 Germany — BaFin

What's happening: BaFin continues active supervision and enforcement of crypto custody licensing requirements under the German Banking Act, now with MiCA overlay creating dual compliance obligations. German custody providers face the strictest regulatory regime in the EU.

So what for deals: Custody providers need wallet attribution, counterparty screening, and asset provenance verification—Reactor for attribution depth, Alterya for institutional custody security monitoring, KYT for continuous transaction screening.

🇳🇱 Netherlands — DNB

What's happening: The Dutch central bank is enhancing supervision of registered crypto firms with explicit focus on AML compliance and Travel Rule implementation throughout H1-H2 2026. DNB's enforcement posture has shifted from registration to active compliance verification.

So what for deals: Dutch crypto firms need real-time travel rule verification and beneficial ownership identification—KYT for travel rule compliance, OSINT for beneficial ownership research on counterparty entities.

🇳🇴 Norway — Finanstilsynet

What's happening: Norway's Money Laundering Act amendments extend AML requirements to a broader range of crypto activities with enhanced VASP supervision launching Q3 2026. This expands the regulated perimeter beyond traditional exchange services.

So what for deals: Norwegian firms offering staking, DeFi interfaces, or custody services previously outside AML scope now need full transaction monitoring—ask "Does your current compliance program cover all crypto activities your firm offers, or just exchange services?"

🇪🇸 Spain — Bank of Spain

What's happening: The Bank of Spain is enforcing strict advertising rules for crypto products requiring prior authorization and prominent risk warnings for all marketing campaigns throughout 2026. Non-compliance triggers immediate enforcement action.

So what for deals: Limited direct compliance technology opportunity, but Spanish banks and fintechs offering crypto products need market abuse surveillance to demonstrate advertising claims are factually accurate—Data Solutions for trading activity verification.

🇸🇪 Sweden — FI

What's happening: Finansinspektionen is implementing enhanced national AML measures for crypto firms beyond EU minimum standards during H2 2026. Sweden is taking a gold-plating approach to MiCA implementation with stricter national requirements.

So what for deals: Swedish crypto firms face dual compliance obligations—ask "Are you building to EU minimum MiCA standards or Swedish enhanced standards, and how will you demonstrate compliance with both?"

United Kingdom (6 events)

🇬🇧 UK — FCA / BoE / HMT

What's happening: The FCA is setting firm Q3 2026 deadlines for existing crypto businesses to submit authorization applications under the financial services registration regime, with final rules and guidance on conduct standards, systems and controls, market abuse, and custody requirements published. Separate stablecoin regulation as payment instruments (distinct from broader crypto authorization) launches Q4 2026, while the Law Commission bill recognizing crypto assets as property under English law progresses through H2 2026.

So what for deals: Every UK crypto firm not yet authorized faces an active authorization application deadline—ask "When is your FCA authorization application due, and what transaction monitoring system are you including as evidence of systems and controls?" The joint FCA/BoE consultation on tokenisation in wholesale markets (closing July 3) shapes the UK's institutional digital asset roadmap—responding positions us with UK policymakers on securities tokenisation infrastructure.

Pipeline trigger: UK banks, broker-dealers, and asset managers exploring tokenised securities need DLT transaction monitoring and collateral management surveillance—Data Solutions for tokenised asset tracking, KYT for post-trade settlement monitoring.

Middle East (6 events)

🇧🇭 Bahrain — CBB

What's happening: The Central Bank of Bahrain is updating its crypto asset module under the CBB rulebook throughout H2 2026, aligning with FATF standards and coordinating with regional Gulf regulators. Bahrain maintains its position as the Gulf's most established crypto regulatory jurisdiction.

So what for deals: Bahraini crypto firms need travel rule compliance and FATF-aligned transaction monitoring—KYT for travel rule infrastructure, Reactor for counterparty screening across regional Gulf transactions.

🇮🇱 Israel — ISA

What's happening: The Israel Securities Authority is implementing a comprehensive regulatory framework for crypto assets increasingly aligned with EU MiCA models through Q4 2026, establishing licensing requirements, investor protection standards, and AML/KYC obligations. Israel's framework convergence with EU standards creates regulatory alignment for firms operating across both jurisdictions.

So what for deals: Israeli crypto firms and banks offering digital asset services need MiCA-equivalent compliance infrastructure—ask "Are you building to ISA requirements only, or EU MiCA standards to enable European expansion?"

🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia — SAMA

What's happening: SAMA's crypto asset regulatory framework implementation reaches completion in Q3 2026 with licensing requirements finalized and applications opening for crypto asset activities. This marks Saudi Arabia's formal entry into regulated crypto markets after years of framework development.

So what for deals: Every crypto firm seeking Saudi market access needs licensing compliance baseline—KYT for transaction monitoring in applications, Reactor for entity risk assessment and counterparty due diligence.

🇹🇷 Turkey — CMB

What's happening: Turkey's Capital Markets Board is implementing a licensing framework for crypto exchanges and service providers throughout Q4 2026, following the 2021 ban on crypto payments. This represents Turkey's return to regulated crypto markets with formal licensing regime.

So what for deals: Turkish exchanges and service providers preparing licensing applications need transaction monitoring infrastructure—KYT for compliance baseline, Data Solutions for market surveillance demonstrating investor protection controls.

🇦🇪 UAE — VARA / CBUAE

What's happening: Dubai's Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority is updating its comprehensive rulebook throughout Q3 2026 with specific provisions for DeFi, staking, and new product types. VARA is moving from framework establishment to detailed product-level compliance requirements.

So what for deals: UAE crypto firms offering DeFi interfaces, staking services, or novel products need risk management systems for activities previously in regulatory gray areas—Hexagate for DeFi smart contract security and transaction monitoring, KYT for staking pool flow surveillance.

Mauritius is tightening its offshore crypto licensing regime.
