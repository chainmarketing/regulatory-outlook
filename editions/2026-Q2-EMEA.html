European Union (21 events)

🇪🇺 EU — EC / ESMA / AMLA

Key developments: AMLA becomes operational in Q3 2026 with direct supervision authority over the EU's highest-risk crypto asset service providers, marking the shift from national-level supervision to centralized oversight for systemically important CASPs. The European Commission's targeted consultation on MiCA review (response deadline August 31) critically assesses whether MiCA remains fit for purpose for DeFi, NFTs, and stablecoins—this consultation will shape the next legislative cycle and determine how decentralized protocols are regulated. Multiple Level 2 Regulatory Technical Standards packages on operational resilience, market abuse, custody, and stablecoin reserves are being adopted throughout Q2-Q4, creating cascading implementation requirements across 27 member states. NFT regulatory clarification is expected by December, determining which NFT activities require MiCA authorization versus exemption as unique/non-fungible tokens. Central Bank of Ireland's DLT and tokenisation discussion paper (June deadline) and ESMA/EBA joint consultation on management body suitability (May deadline passed, but impacts ongoing CASP fitness assessments) add operational governance requirements directly affecting CASP licensing applications.

Deal angle: "Are you operationally ready for AMLA direct supervision starting Q3, and can you demonstrate market abuse detection capabilities required under MiCA Level 2 RTS?"

Product fit: Reactor for entity attribution and CASP counterparty risk scoring under AMLA supervision. KYT for transaction monitoring and travel rule compliance baseline required across all MiCA-authorized CASPs. Data Solutions for market abuse surveillance—critical as ESMA RTS on manipulation detection become enforceable. Alterya for operational resilience requirements under DORA (operationalization July 2026).

🇫🇷 France — AMF

Key developments: French crypto firms operating under PSAN (Prestataires de Services sur Actifs Numériques) registration must transition to full MiCA authorization by July 1, 2026—the AMF is actively urging firms to begin applications as the deadline approaches. PSAN providers offering crypto-asset services before MiCA entered into force can continue operating temporarily, but Q3 marks the hard cutover where firms without submitted MiCA applications face enforcement action. The transition from PSAN's lighter registration regime to MiCA's full authorization requirements (including enhanced capital requirements, operational resilience standards, and custody safeguards) creates immediate compliance infrastructure gaps for 80+ registered French crypto firms.

Deal angle: "Do you have transaction monitoring infrastructure that meets MiCA authorization standards, or are you relying on PSAN-era systems that won't pass AMF review?"

Product fit: KYT for transaction monitoring baseline required in MiCA authorization applications—AMF reviewing real-time capabilities, not retroactive reporting. Reactor for counterparty due diligence and wallet attribution, especially for firms offering custody services under MiCA. OSINT for beneficial ownership verification as AMF scrutinizes management fitness during PSAN-to-MiCA transition reviews.

🇩🇪 Germany — BaFin

Key developments: BaFin continues active supervision and enforcement of crypto custody license requirements under the German Banking Act, with ongoing compliance expectations now overlaid with MiCA custody standards. German banks and crypto firms holding custody licenses face dual supervision—national German Banking Act requirements plus incoming MiCA custody RTS, creating complex compliance obligations that require harmonized monitoring infrastructure. BaFin's track record of enforcement actions against unlicensed custody providers signals Q3-Q4 scrutiny will intensify as MiCA custody standards become enforceable.

Deal angle: "Can your custody monitoring system satisfy both German Banking Act requirements and incoming MiCA custody RTS simultaneously?"

Product fit: Reactor for custody wallet attribution and entity resolution—essential for demonstrating control over customer assets under both German and EU frameworks. Alterya for smart contract security assessment of any DeFi custody integrations, as BaFin scrutinizes technical custody controls.

🇬🇧 UK — FCA / BoE / HMT

Key developments: FCA consultation on final rules for crypto asset authorization closes June 2026, covering conduct standards, systems and controls, market abuse, and custody requirements—firms must prepare Q3 authorization applications as the FCA sets firm deadlines for existing crypto businesses to submit under the financial services registration regime. Separate regulatory framework for fiat-backed stablecoins as payment instruments is advancing through Q4, distinct from broader crypto authorization, creating dual-track compliance for payment-focused firms. FCA and Bank of England joint call for input on tokenisation in UK wholesale markets (closes July 3) shapes the UK's wholesale DLT infrastructure roadmap, covering securities tokenisation, post-trade processes, and collateral management. Law Commission bill recognizing crypto assets as property under English law progresses through H2, enabling improved legal remedies for theft and fraud. Enhanced Corporate Criminal Offences and Transparency Act (ECCTA) enforcement continues affecting crypto company registrations with verification and transparency requirements.
