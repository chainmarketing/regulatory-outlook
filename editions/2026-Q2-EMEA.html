European Union (21 events)

🇪🇺 EU — EC / ESMA / AMLA

What's happening: AMLA becomes operational Q3 2026 with direct supervision authority over the highest-risk crypto asset service providers across all 27 member states—no transition period, no grace window. The European Commission's MiCA review consultation closes 31 August, assessing DeFi treatment, NFT exemption criteria, stablecoin reserve requirements, and CASP operational burdens with potential legislative amendments in 2027.

So what for deals: Every CASP preparing for AMLA supervision needs transaction monitoring that meets group-wide AML/CFT standards before Q3—ask "What infrastructure will you deploy before AMLA identifies you as high-risk?" KYT becomes the authorization baseline, while Reactor enables entity risk assessments at the group level. ESMA's RTS packages on operational resilience, market abuse, custody, and stablecoin reserves create technical requirements that map directly to Data Solutions for market surveillance and Alterya for custody monitoring.

What's critical: The 31 August MiCA review response deadline is the last industry input window before DeFi and NFT regulatory treatment locks in for 2027+. Central Bank of Ireland's DP12 tokenisation consultation (5 June deadline) shapes how DLT infrastructure integrates with monetary stability frameworks—positioning opportunity for firms building tokenised securities infrastructure.

🇫🇷 France — AMF

What's happening: French crypto firms operating under PSAN registration must transition to full MiCA authorization by 1 July 2026. The AMF is urging immediate application submission as the deadline compresses—PSAN providers offering crypto services before MiCA entered into force face authorization requirements with enhanced governance, custody, and AML/CFT standards.

So what for deals: Every French PSAN firm needs compliant transaction monitoring infrastructure before 1 July or faces operating restrictions—ask "How will you demonstrate AML/CFT compliance in your MiCA authorization application?" KYT provides the transaction monitoring baseline, OSINT covers beneficial ownership verification, and Reactor delivers entity risk assessments for counterparty due diligence.

🇩🇪 Germany — BaFin

What's happening: BaFin actively supervises and enforces crypto custody license requirements under the German Banking Act with ongoing compliance expectations throughout 2026. German custody providers face continuous regulatory scrutiny beyond initial licensing—operational resilience, transaction monitoring, and AML/CFT standards remain under active supervision.

So what for deals: German custody firms need infrastructure that demonstrates continuous compliance, not just licensing-stage capability—ask "What monitoring proves to BaFin you're maintaining custody standards post-authorization?" Alterya provides smart contract security for DeFi custody operations, while KYT delivers transaction-level AML/CFT monitoring that satisfies ongoing supervisory expectations.

🇳🇱 Netherlands — DNB
