European Union (21 events)

🇪🇺 EU — EC / ESMA / AMLA

What's happening: AMLA becomes operational Q3 2026 with direct supervision authority over the EU's highest-risk CASPs, ending the era of purely national supervision. The European Commission's MiCA Review consultation (closing August 31) explicitly seeks input on DeFi, NFTs, stablecoin frameworks, and CASP requirements—this is the last window to shape EU crypto policy before legislative amendments.

So what for deals: Every cross-border CASP needs group-wide AML/CFT compliance documentation ready for AMLA's risk-based supervision—ask prospects "How will you demonstrate transaction monitoring coverage across all subsidiaries when AMLA requests group-wide AML documentation in Q3?" DORA's July operationalisation means crypto firms need incident reporting and operational resilience frameworks operational now. The NFT regulatory clarification (December 2026 timeline) creates uncertainty for NFT platforms about whether they need full MiCA authorization or qualify for exemptions—position Reactor for entity classification and KYT for transaction monitoring as the hedge regardless of final ruling.

Product play: Reactor for CASP risk scoring and beneficial ownership mapping across group structures. KYT for continuous transaction monitoring and travel rule compliance. Data Solutions for AMLA reporting requirements.

🇫🇷 France — AMF

What's happening: French crypto firms must transition from PSAN registration to full MiCA authorization by July 1, 2026—the AMF is actively urging firms to submit applications immediately. Firms operating under PSAN that miss this deadline must cease crypto-asset services entirely until authorized under MiCA.

So what for deals: 150+ PSAN-registered firms face a 30-day sprint to demonstrate authorization-ready compliance infrastructure—ask "Do you have transaction monitoring systems that meet MiCA's operational resilience and market abuse detection requirements documented for your July 1 application?" Q4 2026 represents the make-or-break period for firms awaiting authorization decisions while competitors with faster infrastructure deployment capture market share.

Product play: KYT for authorization application baseline demonstrating travel rule and AML monitoring. Reactor for counterparty due diligence and sanctions screening documentation.

🇩🇪 Germany — BaFin

What's happening: BaFin maintains active supervision and enforcement of crypto custody licensing requirements under the German Banking Act, with ongoing compliance expectations beyond initial authorization. MiCA layering on top of existing German requirements creates dual compliance obligations through 2026.

So what for deals: German custodians need wallet attribution and fund flow analysis capabilities that satisfy both Banking Act custody standards and MiCA's operational requirements—position this as "How do you prove chain-of-custody for client assets when BaFin conducts supervisory reviews?"

Product play: Reactor for wallet attribution and source of funds analysis. Alterya for smart contract security assessment of custody infrastructure.

🇳🇱 Netherlands — DNB

What's happening: De Nederlandsche Bank is enhancing supervision of registered crypto firms with explicit focus on AML compliance and Travel Rule implementation during H1 2026. DNB's approach represents one of the more aggressive national implementations ahead of full AMLA supervision.

So what for deals: Dutch CASPs face heightened scrutiny on real-time travel rule verification and beneficial ownership documentation—firms with manual processes or incomplete counterparty data are facing enforcement actions.

Product play: KYT for real-time travel rule verification. OSINT for beneficial ownership identification and verification.

🇳🇴 Norway — Finanstilsynet

What's happening: Finanstilsynet is implementing Money Laundering Act amendments extending AML requirements to a broader range of crypto activities with enhanced VASP supervision during Q3 2026. Norway is moving faster than many EU jurisdictions despite not being an EU member state.

So what for deals: Norwegian VASPs expanding service offerings (staking, DeFi interfaces, new token listings) need to assess whether those activities trigger expanded AML obligations under the amended framework.

Product play: KYT for expanded activity monitoring. Reactor for entity risk assessment across new service lines.

🇪🇸 Spain — Bank of Spain

What's happening: Bank of Spain is enforcing strict advertising rules for crypto products requiring authorization and risk warnings for all marketing campaigns throughout 2026. Non-compliance results in immediate cease-and-desist orders and public enforcement actions.

So what for deals: Spanish crypto firms planning marketing campaigns need transaction monitoring infrastructure that demonstrates they can enforce advertising restrictions (e.g., excluding retail investors from high-risk products)—this is a compliance infrastructure play, not just marketing review.

Product play: KYT for customer segmentation and transaction pattern analysis. Data Solutions for compliance reporting on marketing effectiveness.

🇸🇪 Sweden — Finansinspektionen

What's happening: Finansinspektionen is implementing enhanced national AML measures for crypto firms that exceed EU minimum requirements during H2 2026. Sweden is using MiCA as a floor, not a ceiling, for AML/CFT standards.

So what for deals: Swedish CASPs need to benchmark against both MiCA minimums and FI's enhanced requirements—ask "Are you building to EU baseline or to Swedish supervisory expectations?"

Product play: KYT for enhanced transaction monitoring. Reactor for heightened due diligence on counterparties.

United Kingdom (6 events)

🇬🇧 UK — FCA / BoE / HMT

What's happening: The FCA is setting firm Q3 2026 deadlines for existing crypto businesses to submit authorization applications under the financial services registration regime, with final rules on conduct standards, systems and controls, market abuse, and custody requirements published in June consultation. The FCA/Bank of England joint call for input on tokenisation in wholesale markets (closing July 3) shapes the UK's securities tokenisation roadmap separately from retail crypto authorization.

So what for deals: UK crypto firms face a dual compliance build: authorization gateway requirements for existing business lines and tokenisation framework readiness for securities products. Ask "Do you have market abuse detection systems operational for your Q3 authorization application?" The separate stablecoin regulatory framework (Q4 2026) means payment stablecoin issuers need distinct compliance infrastructure from broader crypto authorization requirements.

Product play: KYT for authorization gateway baseline and market abuse detection. Data Solutions for stablecoin transaction monitoring and reserve verification. Reactor for securities tokenisation counterparty screening.

🇧🇭 Bahrain — CBB

What's happening: The Central Bank of Bahrain is updating its crypto asset module under the CBB rulebook to align with FATF standards and coordinate with regional frameworks during H2 2026. Bahrain's approach focuses on regional harmonisation with UAE and Saudi Arabia.

So what for deals: Bahraini crypto firms need travel rule infrastructure that works across GCC jurisdictions—position this as "How do you ensure travel rule compliance for cross-border transactions with UAE and Saudi counterparties?"

Product play: KYT for travel rule compliance and cross-border transaction monitoring. Reactor for regional entity screening.

🇮🇱 Israel — ISA

What's happening: The Israel Securities Authority is implementing a comprehensive regulatory framework for crypto assets increasingly aligned with EU MiCA architecture, with licensing requirements, investor protection standards, and AML/KYC obligations rolling out through Q4 2026. ISA guidelines now mirror MiCA framework structure.

So what for deals: Israeli crypto firms planning EU expansion need compliance infrastructure that satisfies both ISA and MiCA requirements—this is a standardisation opportunity where one system meets two regulatory frameworks.

Product play: KYT for licensing baseline and AML monitoring. Data Solutions for investor protection and market surveillance.

🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia — SAMA

What's happening: SAMA finalised crypto asset regulatory framework licensing requirements with applications opening Q3 2026. The framework covers comprehensive licensing for all crypto asset activities with FATF-aligned AML standards.

So what for deals: Saudi firms and international CASPs seeking Saudi market access enter application period Q3—ask "Do you have transaction monitoring systems documented for SAMA licensing applications opening next quarter?"

Product play: KYT for licensing baseline. Reactor for entity screening and sanctions compliance. Data Solutions for ongoing regulatory reporting.

🇹🇷 Turkey — CMB

What's happening: The Capital Markets Board is implementing a licensing framework for crypto exchanges and service providers during Q4 2026, following the 2021 ban on crypto payments. This represents Turkey's shift from prohibition to regulated licensing.

So what for deals: Turkish exchanges coming out of gray-market operation need authorization-ready compliance infrastructure built from scratch—this is greenfield implementation opportunity.

Product play: KYT for full transaction monitoring baseline. Reactor for customer and counterparty due diligence. OSINT for beneficial ownership verification.

🇦🇪 UAE — VARA

What's happening: Dubai's Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority is updating its comprehensive rulebook with specific provisions for DeFi, staking, and new product types during Q3 2026. VARA is moving from general VASP licensing to activity-specific regulatory clarity.

So what for deals: UAE VASPs offering DeFi interfaces, staking services, or new token types previously operating in regulatory gray areas now have compliance paths—ask "How will you demonstrate DeFi protocol risk assessment when VARA's updated rulebook goes live?"

Product play: Hexagate for DeFi protocol security and risk assessment. KYT for staking transaction monitoring. Alterya for smart contract security audits.
