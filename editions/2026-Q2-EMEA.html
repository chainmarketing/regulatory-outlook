European Union (21 events)

🇪🇺 EU — EC / ESMA / AMLA

What's happening: The European Commission's targeted consultation on MiCA review closes 31 August, assessing whether the regulation remains fit for purpose covering DeFi, NFTs, stablecoin requirements, and environmental impact. AMLA becomes operational Q3 2026 with direct supervision authority over the highest-risk crypto asset service providers across all 27 member states.

So what for deals: Every CASP preparing for AMLA supervision needs transaction monitoring that meets group-wide AML/CFT standards—ask "What infrastructure will you deploy before AMLA identifies you as high-risk in Q3?" KYT becomes the baseline for AMLA compliance, while Reactor enables entity risk assessments at the group level. ESMA's multiple RTS packages on operational resilience, market abuse, custody, and stablecoin reserves create technical requirements that map directly to Data Solutions for market surveillance and Alterya for custody monitoring.

What's critical: The MiCA review consultation response deadline (31 August) is the last chance to shape DeFi and NFT treatment before legislative amendments—firms silent now lose influence over framework expansion that could bring their activities into scope by 2027.

🇫🇷 France — AMF

What's happening: French crypto firms operating under PSAN registration must transition to full MiCA authorization by 1 July 2026. The AMF is conducting compliance sweeps as the deadline approaches, with PSAN providers offering crypto-asset services before MiCA required to submit complete authorization applications including enhanced AML/CFT systems.

So what for deals: Every French PSAN provider faces a 30-day window to demonstrate compliant transaction monitoring—ask "What system will you show AMF inspectors when they review your July application?" KYT is the authorization gateway, while OSINT proves beneficial ownership clarity required under enhanced governance standards.

🇩🇪 Germany — BaFin

What's happening: BaFin actively supervises and enforces crypto custody license requirements under the German Banking Act with ongoing compliance expectations throughout 2026. German custody providers face stricter standards than baseline MiCA requirements, creating a dual compliance framework.

So what for deals: Every German bank evaluating custody services needs infrastructure that satisfies both Banking Act and MiCA standards—ask "What custody monitoring will you deploy to meet BaFin's enhanced expectations beyond MiCA minimums?" Alterya addresses custody security requirements while KYT monitors transactions flowing through custodial wallets.

🇳🇱 Netherlands — DNB

What's happening: The Dutch central bank enhanced supervision of registered crypto firms with specific focus on AML compliance and Travel Rule implementation during H1 2026. DNB shifted from registration oversight to active enforcement of transaction reporting standards.

So what for deals: Every Dutch-registered crypto firm now faces Travel Rule audit risk—ask "What system generates the Travel Rule compliance evidence DNB will request during supervision?" KYT provides Travel Rule verification while Data Solutions tracks cross-border flows requiring beneficiary information.

🇳🇴 Norway — Finanstilsynet

What's happening: Finanstilsynet is extending Money Laundering Act amendments to a broader range of crypto activities with enhanced VASP supervision measures targeting Q3 2026 implementation. The amendments expand AML requirements beyond exchange services to wallet providers and intermediaries.

So what for deals: Norwegian VASPs offering any crypto service need AML infrastructure covering expanded activity definitions—ask "What monitoring will you implement before Finanstilsynet's Q3 enforcement sweep?" KYT covers transaction monitoring across service types while Reactor enables counterparty due diligence for new categories.

🇸🇪 Sweden — FI

What's happening: Finansinspektionen is implementing MiCA requirements with enhanced national AML measures that exceed EU minimums for crypto firms during H2 2026. Swedish crypto firms face dual compliance with both MiCA baseline standards and stricter domestic AML requirements.

So what for deals: Swedish crypto firms need infrastructure meeting the higher of MiCA or national standards—ask "What system demonstrates compliance with both Finansinspektionen's enhanced AML measures and MiCA authorization requirements?" KYT addresses the stricter Swedish monitoring expectations.

United Kingdom (6 events)

🇬🇧 UK — FCA / BoE / HMT

What's happening: The FCA published consultation on final crypto asset authorization rules covering conduct standards, systems and controls, market abuse, and custody requirements with Q3 2026 application deadlines for existing businesses. The joint FCA/BoE call for input on tokenisation in UK wholesale markets (deadline 3 July) shapes the regulatory roadmap for securities DLT infrastructure, distinct from the retail authorization gateway.

So what for deals: Every UK crypto business needs authorization applications submitted by Q3 with compliant systems demonstrated—ask "What transaction monitoring and custody infrastructure will you show FCA when they review your authorization in Q3?" KYT covers conduct and market abuse requirements, Alterya addresses custody standards, and Reactor proves entity due diligence. The tokenisation consultation creates a second opportunity—ask wholesale market participants "What DLT infrastructure will you propose in your 3 July response to position for securities tokenisation approval?"

What's critical: Separate stablecoin regulation as payment instruments (Q4 framework) means fiat-backed stablecoin issuers need different infrastructure than broader crypto authorization applicants—segment the compliance conversation by asset type and regulatory pathway.
