Executive Summary

A summary of the key regulatory developments across the region this quarter

Twenty-one European Union member states are simultaneously implementing MiCA authorization requirements while AMLA becomes operational with direct supervision authority over high-risk CASPs. The UK's FCA is setting firm authorization deadlines as its crypto gateway closes, and Middle Eastern regulators in UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Israel are finalizing comprehensive licensing frameworks. This is the largest coordinated compliance infrastructure buildout in crypto history—affecting 211 authorized firms across 23 EEA countries, with 149 non-compliant entities facing enforcement.

European Union (21 events)

🇪🇺 EU — AMLA / ESMA / EC

What's happening: AMLA operational launch Q3 gives EU direct supervision over highest-risk CASPs while ESMA publishes multiple MiCA Level 2 RTS packages through Q4 covering custody standards, stablecoin reserves, and market abuse rules.

Deal play: The 211 authorized CASPs need KYT transaction monitoring that scales to AMLA's direct supervision scrutiny and Reactor investigations ready for heightened enforcement—ask whether their current compliance stack can handle supervisor-grade reporting requirements. Three consultations close this quarter: MiCA review (August 31 assessing DeFi, NFTs, stablecoins), ESMA/EBA suitability guidelines for CASP management, and EC anti-corruption strategy covering digital assets. Firms participating need Data Solutions regulatory intelligence to position responses strategically.

What's happening: NFT classification guidance due December determines which activities require MiCA authorization—operational requirements, custody, consumer protection depend entirely on whether specific NFTs qualify as crypto assets versus unique tokens.

Deal play: NFT platforms and marketplaces face binary compliance outcome—ask whether they have Reactor entity verification and KYT flow monitoring ready to implement if their NFT activities fall under MiCA scope.

What's happening: DORA operationalization hits July requiring crypto firms to implement digital operational resilience standards including incident reporting, third-party risk management, and threat intelligence testing.

Deal play: CASPs rushing DORA compliance need Alterya smart contract security assessments and Hexagate protocol monitoring as part of operational resilience frameworks—ask whether their cybersecurity testing regime covers DeFi protocol dependencies.

What's happening: Privacy coin ban under AMLR takes effect July 2027 prohibiting Monero, Zcash, and anonymous crypto transactions—all exchanges must implement full identity verification and traceability or delist privacy assets.

Deal play: Exchanges with privacy coin exposure need 12-month wind-down plans starting now—KYT can identify residual privacy coin flows and OSINT helps verify counterparty identities during delisting transitions.

🇫🇷 France — AMF

What's happening: PSAN-to-MiCA transition deadline July 1 forces firms to complete authorization or cease operations—AMF publicly warning laggards that old registration framework expires with no grace period.

Deal play: PSAN firms still unregistered need emergency compliance sprints—Reactor entity baseline verification and KYT transaction monitoring infrastructure must be operational before July 1 cutoff to support authorization applications.

Deal play: German custody providers face continuous supervision—Alterya custody security assessments and KYT institutional surveillance prove controls meet Banking Act standards during BaFin examinations.

United Kingdom (6 events)

🇬🇧 UK — FCA / BoE / HMT

What's happening: FCA consultation on final crypto authorization rules closes June covering conduct standards, systems/controls, market abuse, and custody—authorization gateway deadlines hit Q3 forcing existing firms to submit applications or exit market.

Deal play: UK crypto firms face binary outcome—complete authorization or cease operations—need Reactor entity verification, KYT transaction monitoring baseline, and Alterya custody assessments operational before Q3 deadline to support applications. Those delaying now likely miss cutoff.

What's happening: Separate stablecoin payment framework finalizes Q4 treating fiat-backed stablecoins as payment instruments distinct from broader crypto authorization regime—creates dual regulatory pathway.

Deal play: Stablecoin issuers and payment processors need KYT payment flow monitoring that satisfies payment services regulation alongside crypto rules—ask whether their compliance architecture handles both frameworks without duplication.

What's happening: Law Commission bill recognizing crypto as property under English law advances H2 enabling improved legal remedies for theft and fraud cases.

Deal play: This removes major institutional adoption barrier—custodians and asset managers can now pursue legal recovery using Reactor investigations and OSINT attribution when theft occurs, making UK custody offerings more competitive versus EU.

What's happening: FCA/BoE joint call for input on tokenization in wholesale markets closes July seeking views on supporting safe adoption for securities, bonds, equities, fund units—covers DLT infrastructure, post-trade processes, collateral management.

Deal play: Banks and asset managers responding need Data Solutions tokenization intelligence and KYT readiness for tokenized securities monitoring—early engagement positions firms for UK's digital securities framework development.

What's happening: ECCTA enhanced verification and corporate transparency measures enforced throughout 2026 affecting crypto company registrations—stricter beneficial ownership and identity verification requirements.

Deal play: UK-registered crypto firms face heightened registry scrutiny—OSINT verifies beneficial ownership chains and Reactor confirms entity legitimacy during ongoing compliance reviews.
