Executive Summary

A summary of the key regulatory developments across the region this quarter

Twenty-one European Union members are simultaneously implementing MiCA Level 2 technical standards while AMLA becomes operational with direct supervision authority over highest-risk crypto firms. UK regulators are setting hard authorization deadlines as the FCA consultation on final conduct rules closes in June, and Middle East jurisdictions from UAE to Saudi Arabia are finalizing licensing frameworks. This represents the largest regulatory infrastructure buildout since 5AMLD—and compliance windows are closing fast.

European Union (21 events)

🇪🇺  — ESMA / AMLA

What's happening: Three critical developments this quarter: AMLA becomes operational in Q3 with direct supervision over highest-risk CASPs across EU27; ESMA publishes MiCA Level 2 RTS packages on operational resilience, custody standards, and stablecoin reserve requirements; and the European Commission opens MiCA review consultation (closes August 31) assessing DeFi, NFTs, and CASP requirements.

Deal play: Ask CASPs which AMLA risk tier they expect and whether their transaction monitoring scales to direct EU supervision—this drives KYT institutional-grade compliance and Reactor entity attribution urgency. For the MiCA review, firms contributing to DeFi consultation need Data Solutions asset classification and OSINT competitive intelligence on policy positions.

🇫🇷  — AMF

What's happening: July 1 hard deadline for PSAN providers to secure MiCA authorization—legacy registrations expire and non-compliant firms must cease operations.

Deal play: Any French CASP still operating under PSAN registration needs immediate KYT authorization readiness assessment and Reactor AML/CFT compliance baseline—June is their last full month to operationalize before enforcement.

🇩🇪  — BaFin

What's happening: BaFin actively supervising crypto custody licenses under German Banking Act with ongoing MiCA alignment requirements.

Deal play: German custodians face dual-regime compliance—ask whether Alterya custody security assessments cover both national banking standards and incoming MiCA operational resilience RTS.

🇳🇱  — DNB

What's happening: Dutch central bank enhancing supervision of registered crypto firms with focus on AML compliance and Travel Rule implementation in Q2.

Deal play: Dutch VASPs under enhanced DNB supervision need KYT counterparty screening and Reactor Travel Rule compliance verification—this is active enforcement, not guidance phase.

🇳🇴  — Finanstilsynet

What's happening: Money Laundering Act amendments extending AML requirements to broader range of crypto activities with enhanced VASP supervision expected Q3.

Deal play: Norwegian firms previously outside VASP scope now face registration—KYT licensing readiness and Reactor entity verification become immediate requirements.

🇪🇸  — Bank of Spain

What's happening: Strict advertising authorization and risk warning requirements for crypto product marketing campaigns throughout 2026.

Deal play: Spanish exchanges launching campaigns need OSINT regulatory precedent research and KYT platform monitoring to demonstrate compliance infrastructure during authorization reviews.

🇸🇪  — Finansinspektionen

What's happening: FI implementing MiCA with enhanced national AML measures beyond EU minimums through H2 2026.

Deal play: Swedish CASPs face stricter-than-MiCA requirements—ask whether their KYT AML controls meet both ESMA baseline and FI national enhancements.

United Kingdom (6 events)

🇬🇧  — FCA / BoE / HMT

What's happening: Four concurrent developments: FCA consultation on final authorization rules closes June (covering conduct standards, custody, and market abuse); firm application deadlines set for Q3 under financial services registration regime; separate stablecoin payment framework expected Q4; and FCA/BoE joint call for input on wholesale market tokenisation closes July.

Deal play: Existing UK crypto businesses without applications filed by Q3 deadline face operational prohibition—KYT authorization gateway compliance and Alterya custody assessment are immediate needs. For tokenisation consultation, institutional respondents need Data Solutions market infrastructure analysis and OSINT regulatory positioning intelligence.
