Executive Summary

A summary of the key regulatory developments across the region this quarter

Twenty-one EU member states, the UK, and six Middle Eastern jurisdictions are simultaneously moving from compliance planning to active enforcement across MiCA licensing, AML supervision, and crypto authorization frameworks. The EU's Anti-Money Laundering Authority becomes operational in Q3 with direct supervision authority over highest-risk CASPs, while France's July 1 PSAN-to-MiCA transition deadline and the UK's Q3 authorization application cutoff create urgent infrastructure buildout windows. This is the quarter compliance shifts from guidance to penalties—buying decisions happen now.

European Union (21 events)

🇪🇺 EU — AMLA / ESMA

What's happening: Anti-Money Laundering Authority goes operational Q3 with direct supervision of highest-risk CASPs across EU27, while ESMA publishes MiCA Level 2 RTS packages on custody, stablecoin reserves, and market abuse through Q4.

Deal play: Regulated exchanges and custodians need KYT institutional surveillance and Alterya custody security assessments before AMLA supervision begins—ask whether their AML architecture can withstand direct EU-level scrutiny versus national regulator oversight.

Three consultations close this quarter: MiCA review consultation (Aug 26 deadline covering DeFi, NFTs, CASP requirements), AMLA RTS on group-wide AML/CFT (May 26 closed, finalization pending), and EC anti-corruption strategy with harmonized crypto penalties (July consultation). DORA cybersecurity operationalization hits July 26 for financial firms including CASPs.

🇫🇷 France — AMF

What's happening: July 1, 2026 is the hard deadline for PSAN providers to transition to full MiCA authorization—no extensions.

Deal play: French exchanges and wallet providers operating under legacy PSAN registration need KYT compliance infrastructure upgrades and Alterya custody assessments immediately—ask if they've submitted MiCA applications or face operational shutdown risk.

🇩🇪 Germany — BaFin

What's happening: BaFin actively enforcing crypto custody license requirements under German Banking Act with ongoing supervisory expectations throughout 2026.

Deal play: German custodians need Alterya banking-grade security assessments and KYT institutional transaction monitoring—ask whether their custody architecture meets BaFin's continuous compliance standards versus one-time licensing approval.

🇳🇱 Netherlands — DNB

What's happening: Dutch central bank enhancing supervision of registered crypto firms with intensified focus on AML compliance and Travel Rule implementation through Q2-Q3.

Deal play: Dutch VASPs need KYT counterparty screening and Reactor investigations infrastructure—ask whether their Travel Rule implementation can demonstrate real-time compliance versus batch reporting.

🇳🇴 Norway — Finanstilsynet

What's happening: Money Laundering Act amendments for crypto extend AML requirements to broader range of activities with enhanced VASP supervision rolling out Q3.

Deal play: Norwegian crypto firms need KYT AML baseline upgrades—ask whether their risk assessment frameworks cover expanded activity definitions under amended law.

🇪🇸 Spain — Bank of Spain

What's happening: Strict advertising rules for crypto products requiring authorization and risk warnings for all marketing campaigns throughout 2026.

Deal play: Spanish exchanges need Data Solutions market surveillance and consumer protection documentation—ask whether their marketing compliance architecture tracks unauthorized promotional activity.

🇸🇪 Sweden — Finansinspektionen

What's happening: Enhanced national AML requirements for crypto firms beyond EU minimums as part of MiCA implementation through H2.

Deal play: Swedish VASPs need KYT AML monitoring exceeding baseline MiCA standards—ask whether their transaction surveillance scales to Swedish-specific enhanced requirements.

🇧🇪 Belgium — RUSI Brussels

What's happening: June 26 speaking engagement on AML/CFT and Travel Rule—industry event signaling Belgian regulatory attention.

Deal play: Belgian exchanges attending should prepare KYT Travel Rule demonstrations and Reactor case study readiness for regulatory conversations.

United Kingdom (6 events)

🇬🇧 UK — FCA / BoE / HMT

What's happening: FCA setting firm Q3 deadlines for existing crypto businesses to submit authorization applications under financial services registration regime, with final rules consultation closing June 26. Law Commission bill recognizing crypto as property under English law moves through H2.

Deal play: UK exchanges and wallet providers need KYT licensing preparation infrastructure and OSINT entity verification before Q3 application deadline—ask whether their systems and controls documentation meets FCA conduct standards or requires baseline buildout. Separate stablecoin payment framework finalization expected Q4.

Bank of England and FCA joint tokenization consultation opens July with call for input on DLT infrastructure and post-trade processes for securities. Enhanced Corporate Criminal Offences Act (ECCTA) verification requirements continue throughout 2026.
