Executive Summary

A summary of the key regulatory developments across the region this quarter

Twenty-one EU markets, the UK, and six Middle Eastern jurisdictions are simultaneously operationalizing comprehensive crypto regulatory frameworks—MiCA Level 2 standards, AMLA's direct supervision authority, and national licensing transitions. The EU alone shows 211 authorized CASPs with 149 non-compliant entities facing enforcement, while France's July 1 PSAN transition deadline and UK's Q3 authorization cutoff create compressed compliance windows. This is the largest synchronized regulatory infrastructure buildout in EMEA history, with transaction monitoring, custody security, and entity screening needs hitting simultaneously across 35 markets.

European Union (21 events)

🇪🇺  — ESMA / AMLA

What's happening: AMLA launches Q3 with direct supervision authority over highest-risk CASPs across all 27 member states, while ESMA finalizes multiple MiCA Level 2 RTS packages on operational resilience, market abuse, custody, and stablecoin reserves.

Deal play: Ask which CASPs expect AMLA designation—those entities need enterprise-grade KYT and Reactor infrastructure before direct supervision begins. European Commission's August 31 MiCA review consultation covers DeFi, NFTs, and CASP requirements—firms submitting comments need Data Solutions to quantify DeFi transaction flows and demonstrate technical implementation feasibility.

Three critical consultations close this quarter: MiCA review (DeFi/NFT scope), ESMA/EBA suitability guidelines for CASP management bodies, and anti-corruption strategy harmonizing crypto-related financial crime penalties. NFT classification guidance (due Dec 2026) determines which marketplaces fall under full MiCA authorization—ask platforms whether their compliance infrastructure handles both exempt and regulated token categories. Privacy coin ban under new AMLR takes effect July 2027—exchanges need Reactor protocol screening now.

Deal play: Existing UK crypto businesses face Q3 cutoff for authorization applications—those without submissions risk operating prohibition. They need Reactor licensing readiness assessments and KYT baseline transaction monitoring for FCA authorization files now. Separate stablecoin framework creates dual compliance requirement—ask issuers whether infrastructure satisfies both payment instrument rules and broader crypto authorization gateway.

Tokenization consultation seeks views on DLT infrastructure for securities—investment firms and custodians need Data Solutions to demonstrate how tokenized asset surveillance integrates with existing market abuse monitoring.
