United States (16 events)

🇺🇸 USA — FinCEN / SEC / FDIC / OCC / DOJ

What's happening: Five parallel regulatory tracks converge in Q3. FinCEN's Bank Secrecy Act reform NPRM closes June 9—this fundamentally restructures how banks, MSBs, and crypto firms design AML programs. GENIUS Act implementation creates three simultaneous NPRMs from FinCEN, FDIC, and NCUA establishing stablecoin issuer AML/CFT requirements with comment periods extending into July-August. Treasury consultation on state-level crypto regulatory uniformity (focus: AML/CFT data principles) runs concurrently with SEC's DeFi broker-dealer registration comment period. DOJ's National Cryptocurrency Enforcement Team expansion targets DeFi protocols, mixer services, and sanctions evasion with enhanced international coordination—enforcement acceleration creates demand for attribution and screening infrastructure.

Deal play: Stablecoin issuers need KYT for GENIUS Act compliance program design and Reactor for reserve transparency—ask: "How are you demonstrating continuous transaction monitoring and sanctions screening to satisfy FinCEN's proposed AML framework before final rules publish?" Banks and MSBs redesigning BSA programs need Data Solutions for risk assessment modernization and KYT for customer due diligence automation. DeFi protocols evaluating broker-dealer registration thresholds need OSINT for participant mapping and KYT for transaction surveillance architecture. Exchanges and custodians preparing for state licensing need Alterya for custody security standards and Hexagate for smart contract risk management as Delaware and other states advance stablecoin bills.

State-level activity matters: Delaware's stablecoin licensing bill and multiple state money transmitter framework updates create compliance fragmentation—Treasury's uniformity consultation attempts coordination but expect 2027 state-by-state licensing acceleration. OCC's digital asset custody interpretive letter update (Q3) and SEC's SAB 121 replacement guidance (Q4) affect national banks and broker-dealers holding customer crypto—custody compliance infrastructure spend accelerates into year-end.

Canada (3 events)

🇨🇦 Canada — CSA / FINTRAC / OSFI

What's happening: Three frameworks tighten simultaneously through Q4. CSA implementing enhanced registration and custody requirements for platforms already operating under securities dealer or marketplace registration—raising operational standards for existing licensees. FINTRAC enforcing FATF Travel Rule with phased compliance deadlines extending beyond tax reporting into beneficiary data verification. OSFI consulting with banks on risk weightings for Group 2a crypto-assets (tokenized traditional assets) under Capital and Liquidity Treatment guidelines—determines whether tokenized securities qualify as collateral in cross-market hedges.

Deal play: Registered platforms need Alterya for enhanced custody standards and KYT for Travel Rule counterparty verification—ask: "How are you demonstrating continuous beneficiary data collection and risk-based verification to FINTRAC ahead of enforcement deadlines?" Banks exploring tokenized asset custody need Reactor for audit trails proving asset segregation and KYT for transaction monitoring integration with existing AML infrastructure. OSFI engagement creates 2027 opportunity for institutional banks evaluating crypto collateral treatment—Data Solutions supports capital requirement analysis and regulatory positioning.
