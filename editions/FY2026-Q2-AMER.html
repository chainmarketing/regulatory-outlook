United States (16 events)

🇺🇸 USA — FinCEN / SEC / CFTC / OCC

What's happening: Three parallel regulatory accelerations hit Q3 2026. First: FinCEN's BSA/AML reform NPRM comment period closes June 9, 2026—this fundamentally restructures how all financial institutions design AML programs under the Anti-Money Laundering Act of 2020. Second: GENIUS Act implementation creates new stablecoin issuer AML regime through simultaneous FinCEN, FDIC, and NCUA NPRMs with 60-day comment windows closing mid-July to August. Third: DOJ's National Cryptocurrency Enforcement Team expansion targets DeFi protocols, mixer services, and sanctions evasion with enhanced international coordination.

Deal play: Stablecoin issuers need KYT for payment monitoring architecture meeting FinCEN's proposed standards and Reactor for sanctions screening—ask: "How are you building your AML program to address FinCEN's risk-based framework before the comment period closes?" Banks evaluating stablecoin custody under FDIC supervision need Alterya for wallet security and KYT for issuer-grade transaction surveillance. DeFi protocols facing DOJ scrutiny need Reactor for mixer fund tracing and OSINT for entity attribution in enforcement response.

Treasury's consultation on state-level crypto regime uniformity and SEC's DeFi broker-dealer registration comment period create regulatory fragmentation risk—platforms operating across state lines need Data Solutions for multi-jurisdictional compliance mapping. Delaware's stablecoin licensing bill signals state banking framework adoption requiring separate licensing infrastructure beyond federal MTL regimes.

OCC's Q3 digital asset custody interpretive letter update and SEC's Q4 SAB 121 replacement create bank custody framework clarity—national banks entering crypto custody need Alterya for institutional-grade wallet security and KYT for bank-grade AML surveillance meeting OCC expectations.
