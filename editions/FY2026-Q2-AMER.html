United States (16 events)

🇺🇸 USA — FinCEN / SEC / CFTC / OCC / DOJ

What's happening: Three critical timelines converge: (1) FinCEN's BSA/AML reform public comment closes June 9—this fundamentally restructures how financial institutions design AML/CFT programs under the AML Act of 2020; (2) GENIUS Act implementation through parallel NPRMs from FinCEN, FDIC, and NCUA establishing AML/CFT program requirements for payment stablecoin issuers under federal supervision—comment periods close mid-July to August; (3) Treasury consultation on state-level crypto regulatory uniformity principles asks how consistent states must be on AML/CFT standards.

Deal play: Stablecoin issuers (Circle, Paxos, PayPal) need KYT for issuer-level transaction monitoring and Reactor for settlement flow surveillance to meet GENIUS Act standards—ask: "How are you operationalizing FinCEN's proposed risk-based transaction monitoring requirements before your comment period closes July 17?" Banks entering stablecoin custody post-SAB 121 replacement (expected Q4) need Alterya for institutional wallet security. We've already engaged Treasury on state uniformity consultation through industry working groups.

What's happening: SEC opened two consultations simultaneously: consolidated audit trail (CAT) requirements for digital asset transactions proposing reporting and surveillance standards, and staff statement on DeFi broker-dealer registration asking when DeFi protocols must register. DOJ's National Cryptocurrency Enforcement Team expanding focus on DeFi protocols, mixer services, and sanctions evasion with enhanced international coordination.

Deal play: DeFi protocols facing broker-dealer registration questions need OSINT for entity identification and KYT for demonstrating non-custodial architecture to SEC—ask: "Can you prove to SEC staff that your protocol doesn't exercise sufficient control over user assets to trigger broker-dealer registration?" Exchanges building CAT-compliant surveillance infrastructure need Reactor for cross-platform manipulation detection and Data Solutions for regulatory reporting feeds.

What's happening: Delaware introduces stablecoin licensing bill under banking framework (September 2026 timeline); multiple states advancing money transmitter law updates and VASP licensing frameworks through Q3/Q4. OCC reviewing digital asset custody interpretive letter for national banks (Q3 expected update).

Deal play: State-chartered banks and trust companies pursuing stablecoin licenses need KYT for state-level AML compliance and Data Solutions for multi-state regulatory reporting—Delaware's banking framework route creates immediate demand for institutional-grade compliance infrastructure differentiated from money transmitter-only approaches.

Canada (3 events)

🇨🇦 Canada — CSA / FINTRAC / OSFI

What's happening: CSA implementing enhanced registration and custody requirements for crypto trading platforms Q3 2026; FINTRAC phasing in FATF Travel Rule compliance deadlines Q4 2026 with requirements beyond existing tax reporting (CRA integration). OSFI consulting on risk weightings for Group 2a crypto-assets (tokenized traditional assets)—whether they can be recognized as collateral in cross-market hedges under Capital and Liquidity Treatment guidelines.

Deal play: Canadian exchanges (Bitbuy, Newton, Coinsquare) need Alterya for CSA-compliant custody architecture and KYT for FINTRAC Travel Rule counterparty verification starting Q4—ask: "How are you operationalizing Travel Rule data collection and VASP verification before FINTRAC's phased deadlines?" Banks holding tokenized securities as collateral need Reactor for real-time market data feeds and Data Solutions for OSFI risk weighting calculations.

Latin America & Caribbean (5 events)

Argentina — CNV

What's happening: CNV crypto asset service provider (CASP) registration regime enforcing phased compliance deadlines through Q4 2026 with AML/CFT requirements aligned to FATF standards.

Deal play: Argentine exchanges (Ripio, Buenbit, Lemon) entering CNV registration need KYT for CASP baseline compliance and Reactor for sanctions screening—Argentina's historic capital controls create heightened regulatory scrutiny on cross-border flows.

Chile — CMF

What's happening: CMF developing implementing regulations for crypto service providers under Fintech Law (Law 21.521, effective January 2023) with licensing and operational framework expected December 2026. Two parallel tracks: VASP licensing requirements and AML-specific provisions.

Deal play: Chilean platforms (Buda, Orionx, CryptoMKT) preparing for CMF licensing need KYT for Fintech Law compliance infrastructure and Data Solutions for regulatory reporting under CMF supervision—ask: "How are you preparing your AML program documentation for CMF's licensing application requirements before December deadlines?"

Colombia — SFC

What's happening: SFC finalizing comprehensive regulatory framework for crypto asset service providers moving from legal gray area to defined licensing regime. Central bank's crypto-asset regulation draft law advancing through Q2 with AML/CFT requirements aligned to 2025 regulatory trends.

Deal play: Colombian exchanges entering SFC licensing need KYT for CASP registration and Reactor for sanctions compliance—Colombia's geographic exposure to illicit finance flows (FARC, ELN, cartels) creates elevated AML enforcement expectations compared to other LatAm markets.

Cayman Islands — CIMA

What's happening: CIMA Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) framework implementation live with registration and AML compliance requirements through Q4 2026. Cayman remains primary offshore domicile for crypto funds and institutional vehicles.

Deal play: Cayman-domiciled funds, exchanges, and custodians need KYT for VASP registration and Alterya for institutional custody meeting CIMA standards—ask: "How are you demonstrating custody control and AML surveillance to CIMA for your VASP license application?" Cayman's role as fund domicile hub creates demand for Data Solutions feeding administrator and auditor reporting.
