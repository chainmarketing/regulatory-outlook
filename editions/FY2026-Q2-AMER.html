Executive Summary

The big picture — where things stand right now

The Americas is executing the world's most consequential stablecoin and AML/CFT regulatory buildout simultaneously. Sixteen U.S. federal and state-level rulemakings converge through Q3 2026—led by FinCEN's GENIUS Act implementation for payment stablecoin issuers and the once-in-a-generation BSA/AML program reform (comment period closes June 9). Canada advances Travel Rule enforcement and custody standards while four Latin American markets finalize VASP licensing regimes before Q4 deadlines. This creates immediate compliance infrastructure demand across stablecoin issuers, banks entering crypto custody, state-chartered money transmitters, and exchanges navigating dual federal-state supervision—a market worth hundreds of millions in monitoring, sanctions screening, and regulatory reporting infrastructure.

What Changed Since Last Quarter

Looking back — what moved, resolved, or escalated last quarter

  • USA — FinCEN ⚡ GENIUS Act NPRMs published for stablecoin issuer AML/CFT programs (comment closes mid-July); BSA/AML reform NPRM comment period closes June 9—first fundamental restructuring of financial institution AML programs in decades, directly impacts crypto firm compliance architecture.
  • USA — SEC/CFTC 📋 Three major consultations opened: consolidated audit trail requirements for digital assets, broker-dealer registration thresholds for DeFi platforms, and SAB 121 custody rule replacement guidance—shaping 2027 compliance baseline for exchanges and custodians.

United States (16 events)

🇺🇸 USA — FinCEN

What's happening: GENIUS Act NPRMs for payment stablecoin issuer AML/CFT programs under FinCEN supervision (comment closes July 17); separate BSA/AML program reform NPRM closes June 9—fundamental restructuring of risk-based compliance for all financial institutions including crypto firms.

Deal play: Stablecoin issuers (Circle, Paxos, PayPal) and banks entering issuance need KYT for transaction monitoring architecture and Reactor for sanctions screening infrastructure—ask: "How are you building AML/CFT programs that satisfy both federal banking regulators and FinCEN's new stablecoin-specific requirements?" BSA reform creates immediate need for compliance program redesign across all registered MSBs and state-chartered transmitters—existing programs built on 1990s frameworks won't satisfy 2027 risk-based standards.

🇺🇸 USA — FDIC / NCUA

What's happening: Parallel NPRMs on GENIUS Act implementation for FDIC-supervised stablecoin issuers and credit union subsidiaries—comment period closes July 17 (FDIC) and mid-July (NCUA).

Deal play: Banks and credit unions exploring stablecoin issuance or custody need Alterya for reserve custody security and KYT for issuer-level surveillance—ask: "Are you building compliance infrastructure that works across all three federal banking regulators simultaneously?"

🇺🇸 USA — SEC

What's happening: Three consultations: consolidated audit trail requirements for digital asset transactions (surveillance standards), broker-dealer registration thresholds for DeFi platforms (comment period open), and SAB 121 replacement guidance on crypto custody accounting (Q4 finalization).

Deal play: Exchanges and broker-dealers need Data Solutions for regulatory classification intelligence and Reactor for audit trail transaction reconstruction—ask: "How are you demonstrating market surveillance capabilities that satisfy SEC's consolidated audit trail standards?" DeFi protocols approaching registration thresholds need OSINT for participant mapping and Data Solutions for protocol activity intelligence.

🇺🇸 USA — Treasury

What's happening: Consultation on principles for state-level crypto regulatory uniformity—specifically AML/CFT data sharing requirements across state money transmitter regimes.

Deal play: Multi-state operators (Coinbase, Kraken, Gemini) need KYT architectures that satisfy both federal FinCEN standards and varying state requirements—ask: "Can your compliance infrastructure generate consistent AML/CFT reporting across 15+ state regulators simultaneously?"

🇺🇸 USA — Department of Justice

What's happening: National Cryptocurrency Enforcement Team expansion targeting DeFi protocols, mixer services, and sanctions evasion with enhanced international coordination.

Deal play: DeFi protocols and custodians face enforcement risk—Reactor provides mixer detection and sanctions screening; OSINT maps protocol participants and governance structures. Ask protocols: "Can you demonstrate proactive sanctions compliance before DOJ enforcement rather than reactive remediation after?"

🇺🇸 USA — OCC

What's happening: Q3 update to digital asset custody interpretive letters—reviewing national banks' authority to provide cryptocurrency custody services.

Deal play: National banks awaiting clarity need Alterya for institutional custody security and KYT for custodian-grade AML surveillance—ask: "Are you ready to launch custody services immediately when OCC finalizes guidance, or will you spend 6 months building compliance infrastructure?"

🇺🇸 USA — States

What's happening: Delaware stablecoin licensing bill under banking framework; multiple states advancing money transmitter updates and Corporate Transparency Act beneficial ownership registries.

Deal play: State-chartered transmitters and stablecoin issuers need KYT for multi-state compliance and Data Solutions for regulatory intelligence on varying state requirements—ask: "How are you tracking which states require registration vs. licensing vs. banking charters for your specific business model?"

We've seen Canadian platforms underestimate implementation complexity—those starting now hit deadlines; those waiting face registration risk.

🇨🇦 Canada — CSA

What's happening: Enhanced registration and custody requirements for crypto trading platforms operating in Canada—Q3 finalization creates new institutional custody thresholds.

Deal play: Platforms seeking or maintaining CSA registration need Alterya for custody security standards and KYT for exchange-grade surveillance—ask: "Are you meeting CSA's institutional custody requirements, or operating on pre-2024 standards that won't satisfy 2027 registration renewals?"

🇨🇦 Canada — OSFI

What's happening: Industry engagement on risk weightings for Group 2a crypto-assets (tokenized traditional assets)—whether they qualify as collateral under Capital and Liquidity Treatment guidelines.

Deal play: Banks exploring tokenized asset custody need Data Solutions for asset classification intelligence and KYT for collateral monitoring—ask: "How are you tracking which tokenized assets OSFI will recognize vs. penalize under capital requirements?"
