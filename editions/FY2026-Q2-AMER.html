Executive Summary

The big picture — where things stand right now

The Americas enters the most aggressive 90-day compliance buildout period in crypto history: U.S. federal agencies publish 11 stablecoin AML/CFT rulemakings under the GENIUS Act while FinCEN's BSA reform closes June 9, triggering $180M+ in transaction monitoring infrastructure upgrades across 400+ banks, exchanges, and payment processors. State-level licensing regimes (California, Delaware, Treasury state framework consultation) create fragmented compliance requirements that only unified entity attribution and multi-jurisdictional screening can solve. Canada's FINTRAC Travel Rule implementation and Latin America's VASP registration deadlines (Brazil Oct 26, Argentina CNV regime) add 85+ cross-border compliance projects through Q4.

What Changed Since Last Quarter

Looking back — what moved, resolved, or escalated last quarter

  • United States ⚡ GENIUS Act shifted from legislation to implementation: OCC, FinCEN, FDIC, Fed, and NCUA published six separate NPRMs (May-Jun) establishing stablecoin issuer AML/CFT programs, CIP requirements, and sanctions compliance frameworks — comment deadlines hit Jul-Aug 2026, final rules effective Q4.
  • United States 📋 FinCEN BSA reform NPRM (Apr 10 publication) fundamentally restructures AML/CFT program requirements for all financial institutions under AMLA 2020 — 60-day comment period closes June 9, 2026, creating immediate urgency for banks and VASPs to assess risk-based compliance framework shifts.
  • Canada 🔄 CSA implemented differentiated regulatory framework for value-referenced crypto assets (VRCAs/stablecoins) on trading platforms, separating stablecoin compliance requirements from other crypto assets — effective Sep 2026, requiring platform-level classification and monitoring infrastructure.
  • Brazil ⚡ BCB VASP grandfathering period ends Oct 2026 — exchanges operating without formal authorization must complete registration or cease operations, triggering compliance infrastructure buildout across 40+ Brazilian VASPs.
  • Latin America 🆕 Multi-jurisdiction VASP licensing acceleration: Argentina CNV registry (US$35K minimum capital), Chile CMF Fintech Law implementation, Colombia SFC comprehensive framework — all converging Q3-Q4 2026 with AML/CFT program requirements mirroring FATF standards.

United States (60 events)

🇺🇸 USA — FinCEN / OCC / Fed / FDIC / NCUA

What's happening: Six federal agencies published coordinated GENIUS Act implementation NPRMs between May-Jun 2026: FinCEN AML/CFT programs, FinCEN/OCC/Fed/FDIC/NCUA joint CIP requirements, FDIC issuer standards, OCC national bank stablecoin supervision, NCUA credit union subsidiary rules, and FDIC BSA/sanctions standards for FDIC-supervised issuers. Comment periods close Jul-Aug 2026; final rules effective Q4 2026 triggering immediate stablecoin issuer onboarding for wallet screening, beneficial ownership verification, and continuous transaction monitoring.

We've already submitted positions on Treasury's state-level framework consultation and OCC national bank stablecoin supervision NPRM.

🇺🇸 USA — FinCEN

What's happening: FinCEN's BSA/AML Act of 2020 reform NPRM (published Apr 10) fundamentally restructures financial institution AML/CFT programs with risk-based approaches, enhanced beneficial ownership requirements, and revised SAR thresholds—60-day comment period closes June 9, 2026. Simultaneously, FinCEN enforcement targeting VASP compliance gaps: enhanced recordkeeping deadline (Oct 2026) requires wallet addresses, blockchain transaction hashes, and beneficial ownership info with SAR thresholds lowered to $3,000.

Deal play: Banks and VASPs face June 9 deadline to comment on biggest AML program restructuring in 20 years. Ask: "FinCEN's new risk-based framework requires you to prove your AML program identifies crypto-specific typologies—can you demonstrate blockchain transaction pattern detection and entity attribution across all customer interactions, or will examiners find gaps in your next audit?" → Reactor for SAR narrative generation with full transaction history; Data Solutions for risk-based program design incorporating crypto exposure scoring; KYT for continuous customer transaction monitoring meeting revised SAR thresholds.

🇺🇸 USA — SEC / CFTC

What's happening: Three parallel rulemakings target DeFi and institutional surveillance: SEC staff statement on DeFi broker-dealer registration (comment period open Jun 26), SEC consolidated audit trail RFI for digital assets (surveillance standards consultation), and CFTC guidance on DeFi commodity trading compliance pathways (Aug 26 deadline). SEC also issued RFI on novel ETFs including crypto asset ETFs—comment period open through Aug 26.

Deal play: DeFi protocols and institutional trading desks: "SEC and CFTC are defining when your protocol constitutes a broker-dealer or designated contract market next quarter—can you demonstrate compliance-ready attribution of all protocol operators, liquidity providers, and user flows, or will you face registration enforcement?" → Reactor for DeFi protocol operator attribution and fund flow visualization; KYT for consolidated audit trail reporting and position limit monitoring; Hexagate for smart contract interaction surveillance.

🇺🇸 USA — OCC / CFTC (Institutional & Custody)

What's happening: OCC requires national banks to implement enhanced risk management frameworks for crypto custody, trading, and node operations (Aug 26 deadline) with capital requirements and prudential standards. CFTC mandates designated contract markets implement digital asset surveillance systems with position limits and real-time market manipulation monitoring (Nov 26). Investment advisers face enhanced custody rule requirements (Sep 26) including qualified custodian verification and segregation.

Deal play: Banks offering crypto custody and exchanges seeking DCM designation: "OCC's Aug 26 enhanced risk management deadline requires you to prove custody security, operational resilience, and AML monitoring for every crypto asset under management—can you demonstrate continuous wallet monitoring, smart contract vulnerability assessment, and qualified custodian verification meeting SEC custody rule standards?" → Alterya for custody wallet security assessment; KYT for custodian transaction monitoring; Hexagate for smart contract interaction risk scoring.

🇺🇸 USA — State-Level Licensing (California, Delaware, Treasury Framework)

What's happening: Treasury consultation on state-level crypto regulatory framework (submitted Jun 26) establishes data similarity principles for uniform state AML/CFT requirements. California crypto laws take effect Jul 26, Delaware introduces stablecoin licensing under banking framework (Sep 26), and multiple states advance money transmitter and VASP registration regimes through Q4.

Deal play: Multi-state VASPs and payment providers: "Treasury's state framework consultation determines whether you need 50 separate compliance programs or one federal-equivalent system—can you demonstrate uniform entity attribution, cross-state transaction monitoring, and beneficial ownership verification meeting both California and Delaware standards simultaneously?" → Data Solutions for multi-state licensing entity mapping and jurisdiction-specific compliance workflows; KYT for uniform AML/CFT monitoring across state regimes.

Canada (5 events)

🇨🇦 Canada — CSA / FINTRAC / OSFI

What's happening: CSA implemented differentiated regulatory framework for value-referenced crypto assets (VRCAs/stablecoins) on crypto trading platforms—effective Sep 2026 with specific terms, conditions, and custody requirements distinct from other crypto assets. FINTRAC implementing FATF Travel Rule with phased compliance deadlines beyond tax reporting (Q4 2026). OSFI conducting industry engagement on risk weightings for Group 2a crypto-assets (tokenized traditional assets) and whether they can serve as collateral under Capital and Liquidity Treatment guidelines.

Deal play: Canadian exchanges and banks: "CSA's Sep 2026 VRCA framework requires you to classify every stablecoin, apply distinct custody and monitoring standards, and demonstrate FINTRAC Travel Rule compliance—can you identify which tokens are VRCAs versus securities, screen counterparties across 180+ jurisdictions for Travel Rule reporting, and prove tokenized asset collateral eligibility to OSFI?" → KYT for VRCA classification screening and Travel Rule counterparty attribution; Data Solutions for VATR generation and tokenized asset risk weighting; Reactor for entity relationship mapping supporting OSFI collateral verification.

Deal play: Brazilian exchanges and banks: "BCB's Oct 26 deadline means you have 90 days to complete VASP registration with full AML/CFT programs and transaction reporting infrastructure, while CVM determines which of your listed assets are securities requiring separate compliance—can you demonstrate continuous transaction monitoring, entity attribution for all counterparties, and securities asset identification meeting both BCB and CVM standards?" → KYT for BCB reporting requirements and CVM securities asset monitoring; Reactor for VASP entity attribution and securities tracing; OSINT for beneficial ownership verification.

Argentina — CNV

What's happening: Argentina's CNV implementing VASP registration regime under General Resolution N°1025—minimum capital requirement of US$35,000 for registry inclusion, effective Sep 26. Small VASPs below threshold escape regulation but larger platforms must complete registration with AML/CFT program documentation.

Deal play: Argentine exchanges: "CNV's Sep 26 VASP registry deadline requires US$35K minimum capital and full AML/CFT program documentation—can you demonstrate continuous transaction monitoring, customer due diligence, and SAR filing capability meeting CNV standards, or will you operate as unregulated small provider with limited market access?" → KYT for AML program compliance; OSINT for registry due diligence and beneficial ownership verification.
