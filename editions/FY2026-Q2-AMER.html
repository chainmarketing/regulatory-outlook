Executive Summary

The big picture — where things stand right now

The Americas enters the most concentrated regulatory implementation period in crypto history. The US GENIUS Act operationalizes July 1 with three federal banking regulators publishing stablecoin AML/CFT rules simultaneously, while FinCEN's Bank Secrecy Act reform fundamentally restructures financial institution compliance programs. Eleven Latin American jurisdictions advance parallel VASP licensing regimes, creating immediate market structure demand from Argentina to Chile. Canada implements differentiated stablecoin trading frameworks by September, while Brazil ends regulatory grandfathering in October—forcing every unlicensed VASP to exit or comply.

United States (38 events)

🇺🇸 USA — FinCEN / OCC / FDIC / SEC / CFTC

What's happening: GENIUS Act implementation dominates—FinCEN's stablecoin issuer AML/CFT NPRM, OCC's national bank supervision NPRM, and FDIC's VASP program requirements all published May-June with July 1 effective date. FinCEN's BSA reform NPRM (comment period closed June 9) fundamentally restructures AML/CFT programs under the AML Act of 2020. October brings FinCEN's full VASP compliance deadline: enhanced recordkeeping (wallet addresses, transaction hashes, beneficial ownership) with SAR thresholds lowered to $3,000. SEC September deadline forces investment advisers to implement enhanced custody rule requirements—qualified custodians, verification procedures, segregation, surprise examinations. CFTC August guidance establishes when DeFi protocols constitute designated contract markets or swap execution facilities, creating immediate compliance pathway demand. DOJ National Crypto Enforcement Team expansion targets DeFi protocols, mixer services, and sanctions evasion with enhanced international coordination.

Deal play: Stablecoin issuers need KYT transaction monitoring and Reactor sanctions screening infrastructure before July 1. Banks entering crypto custody need Alterya qualified custodian frameworks and Data Solutions beneficial ownership verification. VASPs face October recordkeeping deadline—ask: "How are you maintaining wallet address attribution and $3k SAR thresholds?" DeFi protocols under CFTC scrutiny need Reactor to demonstrate compliant transaction surveillance and market manipulation detection. Investment advisers need Alterya custody verification and segregation infrastructure by September.

Canada (4 events)

🇨🇦 Canada — CSA / FINTRAC / OSFI

What's happening: CSA implementing differentiated regulatory framework for value-referenced crypto assets (VRCAs/stablecoins) by September with specific terms and conditions distinct from other crypto assets—all platforms trading stablecoins must implement segregated monitoring. FINTRAC phasing FATF Travel Rule requirements with Q4 compliance deadlines beyond tax reporting, requiring originator/beneficiary data transmission. OSFI industry engagement ongoing on risk weightings for Group 2a crypto-assets (tokenized traditional assets)—whether they can be recognized as collateral in cross-market hedges under Capital and Liquidity Treatment guidelines.

Deal play: Crypto trading platforms need KYT VRCA segregation and monitoring infrastructure before September. Ask: "How are you differentiating stablecoin surveillance from other digital assets under CSA's new framework?" VASPs preparing for Q4 Travel Rule deadlines need Reactor originator/beneficiary tracing and automated compliance workflows. Traditional financial institutions exploring tokenized asset collateral need Alterya custody standards and Data Solutions risk weighting verification for OSFI framework.

Latin America & Caribbean (11 events)

Argentina — CNV

What's happening: CNV's General Resolution N°1025 VASP registration regime now in effect through Q3 2026 with US$35,000 minimum capital requirement. Small providers below threshold escape regulation. Corporate Transparency Act beneficial ownership registries being established at state level with implications for crypto company ownership disclosure.

Deal play: Exchanges and wallet providers above $35k threshold need KYT transaction monitoring for CNV registration and Data Solutions beneficial ownership disclosure infrastructure. Ask: "Are you meeting CNV's minimum capital and ownership transparency requirements?"

🇧🇷 Brazil — BCB / CVM

What's happening: Three critical deadlines: BCB reporting went live May 4 under new resolutions for all registered VASPs. October 31 ends regulatory grandfathering—every unlicensed VASP must complete BCB registration or cease Brazilian operations. CVM consultation (comment period closed June) establishes when crypto assets constitute securities with offering requirements, intermediation rules, and custody standards.

Deal play: Unlicensed exchanges face October 31 compliance cliff—they need KYT reporting infrastructure and Reactor AML monitoring for BCB registration immediately. Traditional financial institutions responding to CVM framework need Alterya custody standards for crypto securities and KYT intermediation surveillance. Ask: "What's your BCB registration status, and can you meet October 31?"

Chile — CMF

What's happening: CMF developing implementing regulations for crypto service providers under Fintech Law (Law 21.521, effective January 2023) with December 2026 target. Regulations will establish licensing and operational framework for virtual asset platforms with AML/CFT requirements.

Deal play: VASPs preparing for CMF licensing need KYT transaction monitoring and Reactor AML infrastructure before December framework publication. Ask: "Are you building compliance infrastructure ahead of CMF's Fintech Law implementation?"

Colombia — SFC

What's happening: SFC finalizing comprehensive regulatory framework for crypto asset service providers with Q2-Q3 2026 publication expected. Central bank finalized crypto-asset regulation draft law, moving sector from legal gray area to defined framework aligned with 2025 AML/CFT trends.

Deal play: Exchanges and wallet providers need KYT transaction monitoring and Reactor sanctions screening to meet incoming SFC framework. Ask: "How are you preparing for Colombia's shift from unregulated to licensed VASP regime?"

Mexico — CNBV

What's happening: Fintech Law continues regulating financial technology institutions including crypto service providers through December 2027. CNBV oversees licensing with AML/CFT requirements while Banxico maintains restrictions on crypto use in payment systems.

Deal play: Licensed exchanges under Fintech Law need KYT to demonstrate payment system restrictions compliance and Reactor AML monitoring. Ask: "How are you maintaining CNBV licensing requirements and Banxico payment restrictions?"

Bermuda — BMA

What's happening: Digital Asset Business Act 2018 provides comprehensive regulatory framework with ongoing licensing requirements, Code of Practice compliance, and BMA supervision through December 2026. Regime covers exchanges, wallet providers, payment services.

Deal play: Licensed digital asset businesses need KYT ongoing compliance monitoring and Reactor to maintain BMA Code of Practice standards. Ask: "How are you demonstrating continuous Code of Practice compliance to BMA?"

Cayman Islands — CIMA

What's happening: CIMA advancing VASP framework implementation through Q4 2026 with registration and AML compliance requirements for all virtual asset service providers.

Deal play: Fund administrators and exchanges establishing Cayman presence need KYT transaction monitoring and Reactor AML infrastructure for CIMA VASP registration. Ask: "Are you building CIMA VASP framework compliance ahead of Q4 implementation?"

Delaware (USA state-level) — State Banking Framework

What's happening: Delaware introduced stablecoin licensing bill under banking framework in September 2026, creating potential state-level regime parallel to federal GENIUS Act.

Deal play: Stablecoin issuers considering Delaware incorporation need to assess dual federal-state compliance—ask: "Are you tracking Delaware's state banking framework versus federal GENIUS Act requirements?"
