Executive Summary

The big picture — where things stand right now

The Americas enters the most significant crypto regulatory implementation phase since 2013 BSA guidance. United States federal stablecoin framework (GENIUS Act) takes effect July 1 with three-agency rulemaking in progress, while FinCEN's fundamental BSA/AML reform comment period closes June 9—both reshaping compliance infrastructure requirements for every VASP, bank, and stablecoin issuer. Canada implements differentiated stablecoin trading rules by September, and Latin American licensing deadlines accelerate across Brazil, Argentina, and Chile. This is infrastructure spend season.

GENIUS Act (July 1 implementation): Three agencies published NPRMs May 26 establishing AML/CFT program requirements for payment stablecoin issuers under federal supervision—OCC for national banks (source), FinCEN for non-bank issuers (source), FDIC for depository institutions (source). Stablecoin issuers need comprehensive transaction monitoring infrastructure before July 1—ask: "How are you meeting the new AML/CFT program requirements across issuance, reserve custody, and secondary market monitoring under GENIUS?" Deal play: KYT for issuer-level monitoring + Alterya for reserve custody security + Reactor for secondary market surveillance.

FinCEN BSA Reform (June 9 deadline): NPRM fundamentally restructures AML/CFT programs for all financial institutions under Bank Secrecy Act and AMLA 2020 (source). Comment period closes June 9—60 days after April 10 Federal Register publication. This affects every bank, MSB, and VASP's compliance program architecture. We submitted our position May 30 (response). Deal play: Banks and VASPs need infrastructure refresh to meet reformed program requirements—KYT + Reactor for enhanced recordkeeping (wallet addresses, transaction hashes, beneficial ownership) with SAR thresholds lowered to $3,000 by Q4 2026.

DoJ Crypto Enforcement Team expansion: National Cryptocurrency Enforcement Team scaling operations through 2026 with focus on DeFi protocols, mixer services, and sanctions evasion—enhanced international coordination with European and Asian law enforcement. Deal play: Reactor for DeFi protocol fund tracing + OSINT for mixer service intelligence + Hexagate for smart contract security verification in enforcement actions.

SEC DeFi broker-dealer guidance: Staff statement on broker-dealer registration requirements for DeFi platforms now in comment period—clarifies when protocols must register as broker-dealers based on control, custody, and order-matching functions. Deal play: DeFi protocols need compliance pathway assessment—KYT for protocol-level surveillance + Data Solutions for regulatory classification mapping.

State-level stablecoin licensing: Multiple states advancing frameworks through Q2-Q4 2026 including Delaware licensing bill (September timeline) and California laws effective July 2026—creating fragmented compliance landscape requiring multi-state monitoring. Deal play: Data Solutions for state regime tracking + KYT for jurisdiction-specific compliance programs.

CFTC DeFi commodity trading guidance: August 2026 guidance establishes when DeFi protocols facilitating commodity trading constitute designated contract markets or swap execution facilities—protocols must demonstrate compliance pathway. Canada (4 events)

🇨🇦 Canada — CSA / FINTRAC / OSFI

VRCA/Stablecoin framework (September implementation): CSA implementing differentiated regulatory framework for value-referenced crypto assets (stablecoins) on trading platforms by Q3 2026—specific terms and conditions distinct from general crypto assets. Platforms need separate compliance infrastructure for stablecoin trading versus other crypto. Deal play: KYT with stablecoin-specific risk scoring + Reactor for originator/beneficiary verification under separate regime—ask: "How are you implementing the CSA's differentiated VRCA compliance requirements versus your existing crypto asset program?"

FINTRAC Travel Rule (Q4 phased deadlines): FATF Travel Rule implementation phased through Q4 2026 for Canadian crypto firms—separate from tax reporting requirements, focused on originator/beneficiary information transmission. Deal play: Reactor Travel Rule solution + KYT counterparty screening.

OSFI crypto-asset collateral consultation: Industry engagement on risk weightings for Group 2a crypto-assets (tokenized traditional assets) under Capital and Liquidity Treatment guidelines—whether tokenized assets can be recognized as collateral in cross-market hedges. Deal play: Data Solutions for asset classification + KYT for collateral flow monitoring.
