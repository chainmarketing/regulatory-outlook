Northeast Asia (2 events)

🇰🇷 South Korea — FSC / KoFIU

What's happening: The Financial Services Commission is developing a framework allowing traditional banks and financial institutions to provide crypto custody services to institutional clients, with implementation targeted for Q4 2026. This represents the first time Korean banking infrastructure can formally offer institutional crypto custody under FSC supervision.

So what for deals: Every Korean bank evaluating custody needs AML/CFT-compliant transaction monitoring before FSC approval—ask "What custody monitoring system will meet FSC standards when institutional clients onboard in Q4?" KYT becomes the licensing gateway, while Reactor enables counterparty due diligence for custody relationships.

🇭🇰 Hong Kong — HKMA / SFC

What's happening: The Securities and Futures Commission continues approving crypto ETFs following 2025's initial launches, with retail access expanding beyond institutional-only products.

So what for deals: SFC-licensed crypto ETF issuers and authorized firms need market surveillance infrastructure—Data Solutions provides the product surveillance and flow monitoring that licensed firms must demonstrate to maintain SFC approval.

Southeast Asia (3 events)

🇮🇩 Indonesia — OJK / Bappebti

What's happening: The Financial Services Authority assumed supervisory control of crypto exchanges from Bappebti and is implementing custody standards for Q3 2026 rollout. This represents the most significant regulatory transition in Indonesian crypto markets, with exchanges now under banking-style supervision.

So what for deals: Every Indonesian exchange under OJK supervision needs custody security infrastructure before Q3 standards take effect—ask "How will you demonstrate custody wallet security to OJK inspectors?" Alterya provides the smart contract security monitoring OJK expects, while KYT covers transaction monitoring requirements.

🇵🇭 Philippines — BSP

What's happening: Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas is enforcing ongoing VASP registration compliance deadlines through H2 2026 following Circular 1108, with unregistered VASPs facing enforcement action. BSP is conducting comprehensive AML supervision of registered VASPs with escalating compliance expectations.

So what for deals: Unregistered Philippine VASPs racing to meet BSP deadlines need baseline AML infrastructure immediately—KYT provides the transaction monitoring BSP requires for registration approval, while Reactor enables entity risk assessment for ongoing supervision.

🇹🇭 Thailand — Thai SEC / AMLO

What's happening: The Thai Securities and Exchange Commission is expanding licensing categories in Q3 2026 beyond exchanges to include crypto brokers and dealers, with enhanced AML requirements across all license types.

So what for deals: Thai firms applying for new broker or dealer licenses need transaction monitoring before Thai SEC approval—KYT provides the monitoring infrastructure for licensing applications, while OSINT enables beneficial ownership verification Thai SEC requires.

South Asia (1 event)

🇮🇳 India — SEBI / FIU-IND

What's happening: The Securities and Exchange Board of India is clarifying through H2 2026 which crypto assets constitute securities under SEBI jurisdiction versus payment instruments under RBI oversight. This jurisdictional boundary-setting will determine which Indian crypto platforms require SEBI registration versus RBI authorization.

So what for deals: Indian platforms need asset classification infrastructure before SEBI's jurisdiction clarification finalizes—Reactor provides the asset attribution and classification capabilities platforms need to demonstrate compliance with whichever regulator claims authority, while Data Solutions enables market surveillance for SEBI-jurisdictional assets.

Oceania (3 events)

🇦🇺 Australia — AUSTRAC / ASIC

What's happening: AUSTRAC transitioned from Travel Rule implementation to active enforcement in Q2 2026, with non-compliant Australian crypto businesses now facing penalties. Separately, Treasury's Tranche 1 consultation is determining how stablecoins and crypto-payment gateways will be licensed as payment service providers under the reformed payments framework, with H1 2026 consultation closing soon.

So what for deals: Every Australian exchange under Travel Rule enforcement needs compliant counterparty data exchange now—ask "How are you meeting AUSTRAC Travel Rule requirements today, not next quarter?" KYT provides the Travel Rule infrastructure AUSTRAC is actively enforcing, while the stablecoin PSP consultation creates a second 90-day window where payment providers need KYT and Data Solutions for payment flow monitoring before licensing.

Second opportunity: Australian payment providers evaluating stablecoin services need transaction monitoring infrastructure before PSP licensing takes effect—Data Solutions provides the payment flow monitoring and KYT the transaction screening that Treasury's reformed PSP framework will require.

🇳🇿 New Zealand — FMA

What's happening: The Financial Markets Authority is advancing FMC Act amendments creating a new regime for crypto asset service providers with AML/CFT and licensing requirements, targeting H1 2027 implementation.

So what for deals: New Zealand crypto firms should begin compliance infrastructure buildout now for 2027 licensing—KYT provides baseline transaction monitoring for future FMA authorization, while Reactor enables the entity risk assessment and AML compliance FMA's CASP regime will require.
