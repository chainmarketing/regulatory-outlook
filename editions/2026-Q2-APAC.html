Key developments: Financial Services Commission's Q4 2026 comment deadline for institutional crypto custody framework creates immediate infrastructure build-out pressure for Korea's 12 largest banks seeking to offer custody services by 2027. The framework requires traditional financial institutions to implement AML/CFT monitoring programs equivalent to existing VASP standards under KoFIU supervision—meaning banks need wallet attribution, transaction screening, and Travel Rule compliance systems operational within 180 days. This directly follows from South Korea's DABA Phase 2 implementation and represents the first major economy enabling full TradFi-crypto custody convergence.

IOSCO's global thematic review puts additional scrutiny on SFC's implementation of investor protection and market integrity standards during Q3-Q4.

Southeast Asia (3 events)

Indonesia — OJK / Bappebti

Key developments: Financial Services Authority's Q3 2026 custody standards enforcement is now operational following regulatory supervision transfer from Bappebti, with 40+ exchanges under direct OJK oversight required to submit first quarterly compliance reports by September. The custody standards mandate segregated wallet architecture, multi-signature security protocols, and transaction monitoring systems meeting OJK's AML/CFT requirements—parallel to global standards but implemented on Indonesia's aggressive timeline. Exchanges that operated under Bappebti's commodity framework now face banking-grade supervision with quarterly attestation requirements.

Deal angle: "What custody security architecture and transaction monitoring systems are you implementing to meet OJK's September quarterly reporting deadline?"

Product fit: Alterya for smart contract security audits and custody wallet vulnerability scanning; KYT for transaction monitoring meeting OJK AML/CFT standards; Data Solutions for automated quarterly compliance reporting workflows.

Philippines — BSP

Key developments: Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas escalated from VASP registration to active compliance enforcement under Circular 1108, conducting on-site and desk audits across 18 registered VASPs through H2 2026. BSP supervision focuses on beneficial ownership verification, transaction monitoring system effectiveness, and Travel Rule implementation—with enforcement actions expected for non-compliant entities. The shift from registration to supervision mirrors AUSTRAC's enforcement trajectory and signals Philippines' commitment to operationalizing AML/CFT standards ahead of FATF mutual evaluation cycles.

Deal angle: "How are you demonstrating transaction monitoring effectiveness and beneficial ownership verification during BSP compliance audits?"

Product fit: KYT for real-time transaction screening and suspicious activity detection; OSINT for beneficial ownership verification and customer due diligence; Reactor for investigating flagged transactions during BSP audit reviews.

Thailand — Thai SEC / AMLO

Key developments: Thai Securities and Exchange Commission's Q3 2026 licensing expansion adds broker and dealer categories alongside existing exchange licenses, with enhanced AML requirements across all three entity types supervised by AMLO. The multi-category framework allows specialization—pure exchanges, brokerage services, and dealer market-making—each with tailored capital, custody, and compliance obligations. This licensing expansion positions Thailand for institutional market development while maintaining retail investor protection through AMLO's AML supervision and Thai SEC's market conduct oversight.

Deal angle: "Which licensing category are you pursuing for Q3, and how are you building differentiated compliance programs for broker vs. dealer vs. exchange operations?"

Product fit: KYT for multi-entity transaction surveillance across exchange, broker, and dealer operations; Data Solutions for license application preparation and ongoing regulatory reporting; Reactor for market manipulation detection under Thai SEC conduct rules.

SEBI's framework development coincides with FSB stablecoin recommendations implementation and IOSCO's global thematic review—positioning India within international regulatory convergence.
