Northeast Asia (2 events)

South Korea — FSC / KoFIU

Key developments: Financial Services Commission opened consultation on framework allowing traditional financial institutions to provide crypto custody to institutional clients, with Q4 2026 comment deadline for implementation. The framework requires bank-grade AML/CFT programs including real-time transaction monitoring, wallet attribution, and enhanced due diligence—elevating compliance beyond existing DABA Phase 2 Travel Rule standards. 12+ major banks including KB Kookmin, Shinhan, and Woori are preparing custody infrastructure to serve institutional demand from pension funds, asset managers, and corporate treasuries.

Deal angle: "Your Q4 custody licensing application requires demonstrating real-time AML/CFT monitoring capabilities—how are you attributing institutional wallet transactions to beneficial owners today?"

Product fit: Reactor provides institutional-grade wallet attribution and entity clustering for beneficial ownership verification; KYT enables real-time transaction monitoring with risk scoring for custody operations; OSINT delivers counterparty screening and adverse media checks for institutional client onboarding.

Hong Kong — HKMA / SFC

Key developments: Securities and Futures Commission continues approving and overseeing crypto ETFs following first approvals in 2026, with retail access expanding beyond accredited investors. SFC supervision now covers ETF issuers, underlying VASP custody arrangements, and market manipulation surveillance across spot Bitcoin and Ethereum products.

Deal angle: "Your ETF surveillance framework needs to trace underlying VASP custody flows—can you demonstrate real-time visibility into exchange reserves backing your products?"

Product fit: Data Solutions provides ETF issuers and custodians with comprehensive exchange reserve monitoring and proof-of-reserves verification; KYT enables underlying VASP transaction monitoring for manipulation detection.

Southeast Asia (3 events)

Indonesia — OJK / Bappebti

Key developments: Financial Services Authority (OJK) implemented custody standards for crypto exchanges under direct OJK supervision following regulatory transfer from Bappebti, with Q3 2026 enforcement beginning. Standards require exchanges to demonstrate segregated custody, multi-signature wallet security, proof-of-reserves, and real-time transaction monitoring. 40+ registered exchanges including Tokocrypto, Indodax, and Pintu face immediate compliance deadlines with OJK audit authority.

Deal angle: "OJK's Q3 custody standards require demonstrating segregated wallet security and transaction monitoring—how are you proving compliance in your first OJK audit?"

Product fit: Alterya provides custody wallet security monitoring and smart contract vulnerability detection for exchange hot/cold wallet infrastructure; KYT delivers transaction monitoring for regulatory reporting to OJK; Data Solutions automates compliance workflow management for custody standards documentation.

Philippines — BSP

Key developments: Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas moved beyond initial VASP registration to active compliance audits under Circular 1108, with ongoing H2 2026 enforcement focusing on transaction monitoring, suspicious activity reporting, and beneficial ownership verification. 18 registered VASPs including Coins.ph, PDAX, and Bloomx now face quarterly compliance reviews with BSP supervision authority to suspend or revoke licenses for deficiencies.

Deal angle: "BSP's H2 compliance audits are reviewing your transaction monitoring accuracy and SAR quality—can you demonstrate risk-based screening of every counterparty?"

Product fit: KYT provides real-time transaction monitoring with Philippines-specific risk scoring and automated SAR generation; OSINT enables beneficial ownership verification and adverse media screening; Reactor delivers fund tracing for suspicious activity investigations.

Thailand — Thai SEC / AMLO

Key developments: Thai Securities and Exchange Commission expanding licensing categories to cover crypto exchanges, brokers, and dealers with enhanced AML requirements under Q3 2026 implementation. New licensing framework requires separate approvals for exchange operations, brokerage services, and dealer activities, with AMLO coordination on Travel Rule compliance and transaction monitoring standards.

Deal angle: "Your Q3 multi-category licensing applications require demonstrating different AML controls for exchange vs. brokerage operations—how are you segregating compliance infrastructure?"

Product fit: KYT provides separate transaction monitoring configurations for exchange, brokerage, and dealer operations under single platform; Data Solutions manages multi-license compliance workflow and regulatory reporting to Thai SEC and AMLO.

South Asia (1 event)

India — SEBI / FIU-IND

Key developments: Securities and Exchange Board of India developing H2 2026 framework clarifying which crypto assets constitute securities under SEBI jurisdiction versus Reserve Bank of India oversight, creating bifurcated regulatory pathway. Classification impacts licensing requirements, custody standards, and AML/CFT obligations—exchanges and custodians must implement different compliance infrastructure based on asset type. Framework follows SEBI's existing oversight of security tokens and investment contracts while RBI maintains payment system and banking regulations.

Deal angle: "SEBI's H2 securities classification framework means you'll need dual compliance infrastructure—how are you mapping your asset inventory to SEBI vs. RBI requirements?"

Product fit: Reactor enables asset-by-asset tracing and classification support for securities vs. non-securities determination; Data Solutions provides dual-regime compliance mapping with separate reporting workflows for SEBI securities and RBI payment system obligations.
