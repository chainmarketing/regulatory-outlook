Northeast Asia (2 events)

🇰🇷 South Korea — FSC / KoFIU

What's happening: The Financial Services Commission is finalizing a framework allowing traditional banks and financial institutions to provide crypto custody services to institutional clients, with implementation targeted for Q4 2026. This opens Korean banking infrastructure to institutional crypto custody for the first time under formal regulatory oversight.

So what for deals: Every Korean bank evaluating custody services needs AML/CFT-compliant transaction monitoring before FSC approval—ask "What transaction monitoring system will you deploy to meet FSC custody standards when institutional clients onboard in Q4?" KYT becomes the licensing gateway, while Reactor enables counterparty due diligence at scale for institutional custody relationships.

🇭🇰 Hong Kong — HKMA / SFC

What's happening: The Securities and Futures Commission continues approving crypto ETFs following 2025's initial launches, with retail access expanding beyond institutional-only products. This positions Hong Kong as the leading Asia-Pacific market for regulated crypto investment products.

So what for deals: SFC-licensed crypto ETF issuers and authorized intermediaries need market surveillance and transaction monitoring infrastructure as retail distribution scales—Data Solutions provides real-time market integrity monitoring while KYT ensures underlying asset custody meets SFC standards.

Southeast Asia (3 events)

🇮🇩 Indonesia — OJK / Bappebti

What's happening: The Financial Services Authority (OJK) has taken supervisory control of crypto exchanges from Bappebti and is implementing custody standards for all supervised exchanges through Q3 2026. This regulatory transition centralizes crypto oversight under Indonesia's primary financial regulator.

So what for deals: Indonesian exchanges must demonstrate custody security and transaction monitoring compliance to OJK by Q3—ask "How will you prove custody wallet security and transaction monitoring coverage when OJK conducts its first compliance review?" Alterya provides smart contract and wallet security infrastructure for custody operations, while KYT delivers the transaction monitoring baseline OJK expects from supervised financial institutions.

🇵🇭 Philippines — BSP

What's happening: Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas continues enforcing VASP registration and AML supervision under Circular 1108, with ongoing compliance deadlines through H2 2026. Unregistered VASPs operating without BSP authorization face enforcement actions as the central bank tightens supervision.

So what for deals: Philippine VASPs still completing registration need compliant AML programs operational before BSP compliance sweeps—Reactor enables entity risk screening for customer due diligence while KYT provides the transaction monitoring infrastructure BSP requires for authorized VASP operations.

🇹🇭 Thailand — Thai SEC / AMLO

What's happening: The Thai Securities and Exchange Commission is expanding licensing beyond exchanges to include crypto brokers and dealers, with Q3 2026 applications opening and enhanced AML requirements applying to all license categories. This broadens Thailand's VASP licensing regime to cover the full crypto intermediary ecosystem.

So what for deals: Thai brokers and dealers need licensing-ready compliance infrastructure for Q3 applications—KYT provides the transaction monitoring foundation for licensing applications while OSINT enables beneficial ownership verification that Thai SEC requires for key function holders.

South Asia (1 event)

🇮🇳 India — SEBI / FIU-IND

What's happening: The Securities and Exchange Board of India is clarifying which crypto assets constitute securities under SEBI jurisdiction versus payment instruments under the Reserve Bank of India's framework, with guidance expected H2 2026. This jurisdictional boundary-setting will determine regulatory treatment for Indian crypto platforms.

So what for deals: Indian exchanges and platforms need asset classification infrastructure before SEBI's guidance finalizes—Reactor enables automated classification and screening of crypto assets by regulatory treatment, while Data Solutions provides market surveillance for securities-classified tokens.

Oceania (3 events)

🇦🇺 Australia — AUSTRAC / ASIC

What's happening: AUSTRAC has moved from Travel Rule implementation to active enforcement, with non-compliant crypto businesses now facing penalties for inadequate transaction reporting. Simultaneously, Treasury's Tranche 1 consultation is finalizing how stablecoins and crypto payment gateways will be licensed as payment service providers under Australia's reformed payments framework.

So what for deals: Australian exchanges need Travel Rule compliance operational now—AUSTRAC enforcement has begun—while stablecoin issuers and payment gateways must prepare for PSP licensing requirements closing this quarter. Ask "Is your Travel Rule infrastructure audit-ready for AUSTRAC enforcement review, and how will you meet PSP licensing standards when the stablecoin framework finalizes?" KYT delivers Travel Rule compliance and real-time screening for both AUSTRAC reporting and future PSP requirements, while Data Solutions tracks stablecoin payment flows for regulatory reporting.

Pipeline note: Dual compliance deadlines (Travel Rule enforcement + stablecoin PSP licensing) position 40+ Australian exchanges and payment platforms in active procurement cycles through September.

🇳🇿 New Zealand — FMA

What's happening: The Financial Markets Authority is developing amendments to the FMC Act establishing a licensing regime for crypto asset service providers with AML/CFT and prudential requirements, targeting H1 2027 implementation. This creates New Zealand's first comprehensive CASP regulatory framework.

So what for deals: New Zealand CASPs have 6-12 months to build licensing-ready compliance infrastructure before FMA's regime launches—early movers gain competitive advantage in the authorization queue. KYT provides the transaction monitoring baseline for future licensing, while Reactor enables entity risk management that FMA will expect from authorized CASPs.
