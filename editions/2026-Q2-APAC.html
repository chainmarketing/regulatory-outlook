Executive Summary

A summary of the key regulatory developments across the region this quarter

Nine Asia-Pacific markets are operationalizing compliance regimes simultaneously across Q3 2026, creating the region's densest institutional procurement window since MiCA passed in Europe. Australia shifts from Travel Rule implementation to active enforcement while Philippines' BSP pushes ongoing VASP registration deadlines and South Korea opens consultation on institutional custody frameworks. Meanwhile, Hong Kong expands retail crypto ETF access, Thailand broadens licensing categories, and Indonesia transfers exchange supervision to OJK with new custody standards—all requiring immediate transaction monitoring, entity screening, and compliance infrastructure.

Northeast Asia (2 events)

Hong Kong — HKMA / SFC

What's happening: SFC continuing retail crypto ETF approvals following first-wave launches—retail access expanding beyond accredited investors into mass-market distribution through licensed intermediaries.

Deal play: Reactor screens ETF underlying asset risk and counterparty exposure for fund managers and distributors navigating SFC ongoing supervision requirements.

South Korea — FSC / KoFIU

What's happening: FSC developing institutional custody framework for traditional financial institutions (banks, securities firms) to provide crypto custody to institutional clients—consultation runs H2 2026 with Q4 implementation target.

Deal play: KYT establishes transaction monitoring for banks entering custody market; question is "How do you meet AML/CFT requirements for institutional crypto custody under FSC supervision?"—this expands compliance scope beyond existing DABA exchange regime into traditional finance.

Southeast Asia (3 events)

Indonesia — OJK / Bappebti

What's happening: OJK implementing custody standards for crypto exchanges following regulatory transfer from Bappebti—Financial Services Authority now primary supervisor with Q3 2026 compliance deadlines for custody infrastructure.

Deal play: Alterya provides smart contract security and custody infrastructure assessment for exchanges meeting OJK standards; KYT establishes baseline transaction monitoring under new supervisory regime.

Philippines — BSP

What's happening: BSP implementing comprehensive VASP registration and AML supervision following Circular 1108—rolling compliance deadlines through H2 2026 with Bangko Sentral exercising direct supervision authority over registered VASPs.

Deal play: KYT baseline for VASP registration applications and ongoing compliance; Reactor provides entity risk assessment for BSP fitness and propriety reviews during registration process.

Thailand — Thai SEC / AMLO

What's happening: Thai SEC expanding licensing categories for crypto exchanges, brokers, and dealers—enhanced AML requirements apply across all license types with Q3 implementation.

Deal play: KYT multi-license monitoring for firms operating across categories; Reactor screens broker and dealer counterparties as licensing regime broadens beyond pure exchange supervision.

South Asia (1 event)

India — SEBI / FIU-IND

What's happening: SEBI clarifying which crypto assets constitute securities under SEBI jurisdiction versus RBI banking/payments authority—regulatory boundary determination affects compliance requirements for exchanges, brokers, and custody providers through H2 2026.

Deal play: Reactor provides asset classification and entity routing based on SEBI vs. RBI jurisdictional determinations; question is "How do you ensure transaction monitoring routes assets to correct regulatory framework?"—securities compliance differs fundamentally from payments compliance.

Oceania (3 events)

Australia — AUSTRAC / ASIC

What's happening: Two parallel frameworks converging Q3: AUSTRAC moved from Travel Rule implementation to active enforcement (non-compliant businesses face penalties), while Treasury consultation on stablecoin payment licensing under reformed PSP framework closes H1 with legislation expected Q4.

Deal play: KYT Travel Rule compliance for AUSTRAC enforcement; Reactor screens stablecoin issuers and crypto-payment gateways for PSP licensing applications under Treasury framework—banks need to assess which crypto counterparties meet modern PSP requirements.

New Zealand — FMA

What's happening: FMC Act amendments for crypto establish new VASP regime with AML/CFT and licensing requirements—implementation H1 2027 but FMA pre-implementation guidance expected Q4 2026.

Deal play: KYT licensing readiness for firms planning FMC Act applications; Reactor provides CASP due diligence for institutional counterparties preparing for H1 2027 regime launch.
