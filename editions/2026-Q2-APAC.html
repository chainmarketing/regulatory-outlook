Executive Summary

A summary of the key regulatory developments across the region this quarter

Nine Asia-Pacific markets are operationalizing new compliance frameworks this quarter, with Australia moving to active Travel Rule enforcement, South Korea developing institutional custody standards, and the Philippines implementing comprehensive VASP supervision. The region splits between mature licensing regimes (Hong Kong, Thailand) expanding product categories and emerging markets (Indonesia, Philippines) establishing baseline AML infrastructure. This creates distinct procurement cycles: upgrade opportunities in Northeast Asia and Oceania, greenfield implementations across Southeast Asia.

What Changed Since Last Quarter

Looking back — what moved since the last edition

  • Australia ⚡ AUSTRAC shifted from implementation to active Travel Rule enforcement—compliance window closed, audit cycle begins. Treasury consultation on stablecoin payment licensing (H1 deadline) will reclassify crypto gateways as PSPs, triggering KYT requirement cascades across payment processors.
  • South Korea 🆕 FSC opened institutional custody framework development (Q4 delivery)—traditional financial institutions can now offer crypto custody to institutional clients, creating new market segment requiring enterprise-grade transaction monitoring and compliance infrastructure.
  • Philippines 🏛️ BSP Circular 1108 VASP registration moves into ongoing supervision phase with rolling compliance deadlines through H2—direct AML supervision by central bank represents enforcement escalation beyond registration-only regimes elsewhere in Southeast Asia.
  • Indonesia 🔄 OJK finalizes custody standards for crypto exchanges (Q3 implementation) following regulatory authority transfer from Bappebti—exchanges must upgrade custody security and risk management systems to banking-sector standards.
  • Thailand 📋 Thai SEC expanding licensing categories beyond exchanges to brokers and dealers (Q3)—enhanced AML requirements apply across all license types, widening compliance perimeter and creating broker-dealer upgrade cycle.

Northeast Asia (2 events)

Hong Kong — SFC

What's happening: SFC continuing crypto ETF approvals with retail access expansion following first wave—ongoing oversight regime for fund managers and market makers operational.

Deal play: Reactor entity screening for ETF counterparties and OSINT fund manager ownership verification—ask: "How do you verify authorized participant compliance and underlying crypto custody arrangements?"

South Korea — FSC / KoFIU

What's happening: FSC developing framework for traditional financial institutions to provide institutional crypto custody (Q4 delivery)—banks and securities firms can offer custody to corporate and institutional clients under enhanced AML/CFT programs.

Deal play: KYT enterprise transaction monitoring for institutional custody platforms—ask: "When custody goes live Q4, how will you monitor institutional client transaction patterns and demonstrate continuous AML compliance to FSC?" Coordinate with Legal on stablecoin issuer custody requirements under this framework.

Southeast Asia (3 events)

Indonesia — OJK / Bappebti

What's happening: OJK implementing custody standards for crypto exchanges (Q3) following regulatory transfer—exchanges must meet banking-sector custody security, segregation, and risk management requirements.

Deal play: Alterya custody security assessment and KYT monitoring integration—ask: "How will you demonstrate custody controls meet OJK banking standards and provide continuous transaction oversight?" Brief GTM NAM on banking customer requirements emerging from this transition.

Philippines — BSP

What's happening: BSP implementing comprehensive VASP registration and direct AML supervision under Circular 1108 with ongoing compliance deadlines through H2—central bank conducting examinations and enforcement actions against non-compliant VASPs.

Deal play: KYT baseline AML compliance and Reactor entity verification for VASP registration—ask: "BSP is moving beyond registration to active supervision—can you demonstrate continuous transaction monitoring and suspicious activity detection to examination teams?" Assess scope of direct supervision powers and brief EU operations on highest-risk crypto asset requirements.

Thailand — Thai SEC / AMLO

What's happening: Thai SEC expanding licensing categories (Q3) to cover crypto brokers and dealers beyond exchanges—enhanced AML requirements apply uniformly across all license types.

Deal play: KYT multi-license monitoring architecture—ask: "How will you adapt compliance infrastructure when broker-dealer licenses launch Q3 and you're operating across multiple regulatory categories simultaneously?"

South Asia (1 event)

India — SEBI / FIU-IND

What's happening: SEBI clarifying which crypto assets constitute securities under its jurisdiction versus RBI oversight (H2)—jurisdictional boundaries between securities regulator and central bank being formalized.

Deal play: KYT product segmentation by regulatory classification and Reactor jurisdiction mapping—ask: "How will you classify crypto assets between SEBI and RBI oversight, and can your compliance infrastructure adapt when classifications change?" Track FSB stablecoin recommendations for alignment signals.

Oceania (3 events)

Australia — AUSTRAC / ASIC

What's happening: AUSTRAC moved from Travel Rule implementation to active enforcement—audit cycle targeting Australian crypto businesses begins. Treasury consultation on stablecoin and crypto-payment gateway licensing as PSPs under reformed payments framework (H1 deadline closes soon).

Deal play: KYT Travel Rule compliance verification and Reactor counterparty screening—ask: "AUSTRAC enforcement is live—can you demonstrate Travel Rule compliance in real-time, and are you preparing for stablecoin PSP licensing requirements that will expand KYT obligations across payment flows?" Stablecoin payment licensing directly affects KYT requirements for Australian PSPs—track deadline closely.

New Zealand — FMA

What's happening: FMC Act amendments for crypto (H1 2027 implementation)—new licensing regime for crypto asset service providers with comprehensive AML/CFT and operational requirements.

Deal play: KYT licensing preparation and Reactor readiness assessment for 2027 regime—ask: "FMC Act goes live H1 2027—are you building compliance infrastructure now or waiting until licensing opens? Early movers secure competitive advantage in FMA approval queue."
