Sub-Regional Breakdown

Northeast Asia (2 events)

South Korea — FSC / KoFIU

Key developments: Financial Services Commission's H2 2026 framework enabling traditional financial institutions to provide crypto custody to institutional clients enters consultation phase with Q4 2026 comment deadline. Framework requires bank-grade AML/CFT programs for custodians, triggering immediate wallet attribution and transaction monitoring infrastructure requirements for 12+ institutions positioning for custody licenses. This directly builds on existing DABA Phase 2 Travel Rule infrastructure but elevates compliance standards to institutional banking grade.

Deal angle: "When FSC finalizes custody rules in Q4, can your institution demonstrate real-time monitoring of institutional wallet activity and attribution of counterparty exposure across your custody book?"

Product fit: Reactor for institutional wallet attribution and beneficial ownership mapping; KYT for continuous custody transaction monitoring meeting FSC's bank-grade AML/CFT standards; Data Solutions for entity classification across institutional counterparties.

Hong Kong — HKMA / SFC

Key developments: Securities and Futures Commission continues expanding crypto ETF approvals with retail access broadening beyond initial institutional-only products. SFC oversight now covers both spot ETF providers and underlying VASP custodians, creating dual-layer compliance requirements for asset managers and their crypto service providers.

Deal angle: "As SFC expands retail crypto ETF access, how are you monitoring underlying VASP exposure and ensuring NAV calculation integrity across your ETF custody chain?"

Product fit: Data Solutions for ETF constituent monitoring and VASP exposure tracking; KYT for underlying custody wallet surveillance ensuring SFC-compliant asset safeguarding.

Southeast Asia (3 events)

Indonesia — OJK / Bappebti

Key developments: Financial Services Authority (OJK) implementing custody standards for crypto exchanges under its supervision following regulatory transfer from Bappebti, with Q3 2026 enforcement beginning this quarter. 40+ exchanges under OJK supervision must demonstrate compliant custody infrastructure including segregated wallet architecture, cold storage protocols, and transaction monitoring capabilities meeting OJK's institutional custody standards.

Deal angle: "OJK custody standards enforcement starts this quarter — can you demonstrate segregated wallet monitoring, cold storage attestation, and transaction surveillance meeting OJK's institutional requirements?"

Product fit: Alterya for exchange wallet security assessment and custody infrastructure validation; KYT for transaction monitoring across hot/cold wallet architecture; Reactor for wallet attribution demonstrating segregation compliance.

Philippines — BSP

Key developments: Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas implementing comprehensive VASP registration and AML supervision following Circular 1108 with ongoing compliance deadlines through H2 2026. BSP moved from registration phase to active compliance audits, conducting direct VASP monitoring with focus on transaction monitoring infrastructure, beneficial ownership verification, and Travel Rule readiness ahead of expected H2 implementation.

Deal angle: "BSP is now conducting direct VASP compliance audits — can you demonstrate continuous transaction monitoring, beneficial ownership tracing, and Travel Rule readiness before BSP's next audit cycle?"

Product fit: KYT for transaction monitoring meeting BSP's Circular 1108 requirements; OSINT for beneficial ownership verification and customer due diligence; Reactor for Travel Rule counterparty attribution.

Thailand — Thai SEC / AMLO

Key developments: Securities and Exchange Commission expanding licensing categories and supervision of crypto exchanges, brokers, and dealers with enhanced AML requirements effective Q3 2026. Multi-category licensing creates differentiated compliance requirements across business models, with broker-dealers facing heightened transaction monitoring and market surveillance obligations beyond exchange-only operators.

Deal angle: "Thai SEC's Q3 licensing expansion means broker-dealers face different AML requirements than exchanges — does your compliance infrastructure differentiate monitoring across your business lines?"

Product fit: KYT for transaction monitoring across differentiated business models; Data Solutions for entity classification supporting license-specific compliance; Hexagate for smart contract security as DeFi integration expands under new licensing categories.

South Asia (1 event)

India — SEBI / FIU-IND

Key developments: Securities and Exchange Board of India clarifying which crypto assets constitute securities and fall under SEBI jurisdiction versus RBI oversight, with framework expected H2 2026. Classification impacts 20+ Indian exchanges determining which products require securities licensing versus payment system authorization, creating urgent product rationalization and compliance segregation requirements as framework deadline approaches.

Deal angle: "SEBI's H2 classification framework will determine whether your crypto products require securities licensing or RBI payment authorization — can you trace asset flows across both jurisdictions?"

Product fit: Reactor for asset tracing demonstrating securities versus payment use cases; Data Solutions for entity classification supporting jurisdiction-specific compliance; KYT for transaction monitoring across SEBI-regulated versus RBI-supervised products.

Oceania (3 events)

Australia — AUSTRAC / ASIC

Key developments: AUSTRAC moving from Travel Rule implementation phase to active enforcement with compliance audits targeting 90+ registered DCEs, while Australian Treasury's Tranche 1 Payments Licensing Legislation consultation (H1 2026 deadline) establishes how stablecoins and crypto-payment gateways will be licensed as payment service providers under reformed payments framework. Dual enforcement timeline creates parallel compliance tracks — Travel Rule remediation and stablecoin PSP licensing preparation — both requiring enhanced transaction monitoring and entity screening infrastructure.

Deal angle: "AUSTRAC Travel Rule audits are active now while Treasury's stablecoin PSP consultation deadline hits this quarter — can your compliance infrastructure handle both Travel Rule counterparty screening and stablecoin payment classification simultaneously?"

Product fit: KYT for Travel Rule real-time screening and stablecoin payment transaction monitoring; Data Solutions for PSP entity classification and Travel Rule VASP identification; Reactor for counterparty attribution across both compliance regimes.

New Zealand — FMA

Key developments: Financial Markets Authority FMC Act amendments for crypto establishing new regime for crypto asset service providers with AML/CFT and licensing requirements, with H1 2027 implementation timeline. FMA framework closely mirrors Australian approach but with New Zealand-specific licensing categories, creating cross-Tasman compliance arbitrage opportunities for providers operating in both markets.

Deal angle: "FMA's 2027 crypto regime mirrors AUSTRAC's Travel Rule framework — are you building compliance infrastructure that scales across both Australia and New Zealand?"

Product fit: KYT for transaction monitoring meeting FMA's AML/CFT requirements; Data Solutions for cross-jurisdiction entity classification supporting trans-Tasman compliance strategies.
