Sub-Regional Breakdown

Northeast Asia (2 events)

South Korea — FSC / KoFIU

Key developments: Financial Services Commission's H2 2026 framework enabling traditional financial institutions to provide crypto custody to institutional clients enters consultation phase with Q4 2026 comment deadline. Framework requires bank-grade AML/CFT programs for custodians, triggering immediate wallet attribution and transaction monitoring infrastructure requirements for 12+ institutions positioning for custody licenses.

Deal angle: "When FSC finalizes custody rules in Q4, can your institution demonstrate real-time monitoring of institutional wallet activity and attribution of counterparty exposure across your custody book?"

Product fit: Reactor for institutional wallet attribution and beneficial ownership mapping; KYT for continuous custody transaction monitoring; Data Solutions for entity classification across institutional counterparties.

Hong Kong — HKMA / SFC

Key developments: Securities and Futures Commission continues expanding crypto ETF approvals following initial product launches, with retail access broadening through Q3 2026. SFC oversight focuses on constituent asset monitoring, custody arrangements, and market manipulation surveillance for approved products.

Deal angle: "How do you demonstrate to SFC that your ETF custody and constituent monitoring meets securities-grade surveillance standards?"

Product fit: Data Solutions for ETF constituent asset tracking and exposure monitoring; KYT for VASP surveillance and custody wallet screening.

Southeast Asia (3 events)

Indonesia — OJK / Bappebti

Key developments: Financial Services Authority implementing custody standards for crypto exchanges under OJK supervision following full regulatory transfer from Bappebti in Q3 2026. New standards require exchange hot/cold wallet segregation monitoring, proof-of-reserves verification, and real-time custody transaction reporting to OJK for 25+ licensed exchanges.

Deal angle: "When OJK audits your custody infrastructure next quarter, can you produce real-time hot wallet monitoring, cold storage verification, and transaction-level custody reporting?"

Product fit: Alterya for smart contract custody security and wallet vulnerability monitoring; KYT for custody transaction surveillance and OJK regulatory reporting; Hexagate for exchange wallet security posture assessment.

Philippines — BSP

Key developments: Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas moved Circular 1108 from registration phase to active VASP supervision with ongoing compliance audits through H2 2026. BSP conducting transaction monitoring reviews, AML program assessments, and customer due diligence audits across 15+ registered VASPs, creating immediate compliance infrastructure gaps.

Deal angle: "Can you demonstrate to BSP auditors that your VASP transaction monitoring covers beneficial ownership attribution, sanctions screening, and suspicious activity detection across your customer base?"

Product fit: KYT for comprehensive BSP transaction monitoring and STR filing; OSINT for VASP registration due diligence and beneficial ownership verification; Data Solutions for customer wallet classification and risk scoring.

Thailand — Thai SEC / AMLO

Key developments: Thai SEC expanding licensing framework to cover exchanges, brokers, and dealers with separate license categories and enhanced AML requirements enforced by Q3 2026. Multi-category structure requires tailored transaction monitoring for trading platforms vs. advisory services vs. dealer activities, creating compliance segmentation across 18+ license applicants.

Deal angle: "How do you customize transaction monitoring for Thai SEC's multi-category licensing structure—can you differentiate broker, dealer, and exchange activity surveillance?"

Product fit: KYT for category-specific transaction monitoring and Thai SEC reporting; Data Solutions for license classification and entity categorization across counterparties.

South Asia (1 event)

India — SEBI / FIU-IND

Key developments: Securities and Exchange Board of India clarifying H2 2026 which crypto assets constitute securities under SEBI jurisdiction versus RBI oversight, creating regulatory boundary definitions for exchanges, custody providers, and asset managers. Classification framework determines which entities require securities licensing vs. VASP registration, triggering compliance infrastructure bifurcation.

Deal angle: "When SEBI finalizes securities classification, can you demonstrate that your transaction monitoring correctly categorizes asset types and applies appropriate securities surveillance vs. VASP AML controls?"

Product fit: Reactor for securities asset tracing and beneficial ownership investigation; Data Solutions for asset classification and jurisdiction mapping; KYT for bifurcated monitoring across securities vs. non-securities crypto assets.

Key developments: AUSTRAC transitioned to active Travel Rule enforcement in Q2 2026, now conducting compliance audits across 90+ registered DCEs requiring demonstrated real-time counterparty screening and VATR reporting by Q3. Simultaneously, Treasury's Tranche 1 consultation on stablecoin PSP licensing closes H1 2026, determining which payment providers require licensing under reformed payments framework—creating dual compliance pressure on exchanges and payment gateways.

Deal angle: "When AUSTRAC audits your Travel Rule compliance next quarter, can you demonstrate real-time beneficiary screening across 180+ jurisdictions and attribute every counterparty wallet to a known entity?"

Planning phase through Q3-Q4 2026 creates positioning window for pre-compliance infrastructure adoption by 8+ prospective VASP licensees.

Deal angle: "Are you building FMA-ready transaction monitoring infrastructure now, or waiting until 2027 when competitors already have operational compliance programs?"

Product fit: KYT for pre-compliance VASP transaction monitoring positioning; Reactor for service provider attribution and beneficial ownership mapping; OSINT for license application due diligence.
