Oceania (3 events)

🇦🇺 Australia — AUSTRAC / ASIC

What's happening: AUSTRAC moved from Travel Rule implementation to active enforcement against Australian crypto businesses in Q2 2026. Treasury's Tranche 1 Package consultation (H1 2026 deadline) defines how stablecoins and crypto-payment gateways will be licensed as payment service providers under the reformed payments framework.

So what for deals: Every Australian crypto business now operates under live Travel Rule enforcement—ask "What transaction monitoring infrastructure are you using to demonstrate AUSTRAC compliance today?" KYT is the baseline for Travel Rule counterparty screening, while the stablecoin PSP licensing consultation creates urgency for payment gateway operators who need compliant infrastructure before final rules lock in Q4. Reactor enables the entity due diligence that PSP licensing applications will require.

Critical for pipeline: The consultation deadline means payment gateway operators and stablecoin issuers are evaluating compliance infrastructure NOW—before licensing requirements finalize. Data Solutions positions Chainalysis for market surveillance requirements that will follow PSP licensing.

🇳🇿 New Zealand — FMA

What's happening: FMA is advancing Financial Markets Conduct Act amendments for H1 2027 implementation, creating a new regime for crypto asset service providers with AML/CFT and licensing requirements.

So what for deals: New Zealand CASPs have 12 months to prepare for licensing—ask "What compliance infrastructure will you deploy before FMA licensing goes live?" KYT and OSINT become table stakes for the licensing application baseline, positioning Chainalysis early in the preparation cycle before the 2027 rush.

🇰🇷 South Korea — FSC / KoFIU

What's happening: FSC is developing a Q4 2026 framework enabling traditional financial institutions to provide crypto custody services to institutional clients. This represents Korea's first regulated pathway for banks and securities firms to enter crypto custody.

So what for deals: Every Korean bank evaluating institutional crypto custody needs transaction monitoring that meets FSC and KoFIU AML/CFT standards—ask "What custody monitoring infrastructure will you deploy before FSC approves your institutional crypto custody license?" KYT is the baseline for custody transaction monitoring, while Alterya provides the smart contract and wallet security that institutional custody demands. This is a greenfield opportunity: Korean banks building crypto custody from scratch need compliant infrastructure before Q4 licensing.

🇭🇰 Hong Kong — HKMA / SFC

So what for deals: Hong Kong crypto ETF issuers and authorized intermediaries need counterparty and custody risk monitoring—ask "How are you screening the VASPs and custodians your ETF relies on?"
