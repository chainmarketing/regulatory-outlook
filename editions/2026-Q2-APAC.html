Executive Summary

A summary of the key regulatory developments across the region this quarter

Nine Asia-Pacific markets are simultaneously moving from travel rule implementation to enforcement, VASP licensing deadlines to active supervision, and retail-only frameworks to institutional custody access. Australia's AUSTRAC has shifted to penalty enforcement while South Korea opens traditional financial institutions to crypto custody services. The compliance infrastructure buildout represents the region's largest transaction monitoring and custody security opportunity since initial licensing regimes launched—and Q3 deadlines mean buying windows close fast.
